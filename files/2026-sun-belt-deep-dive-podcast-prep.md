# 2026 Sun Belt Football Deep Dive — Podcast Prep

*Compiled July 19, 2026. Sources synthesized: ESPN/Bill Connelly's Sun Belt preview + SP+ projections (May 15), current FanDuel/market numbers on the Sun Belt title and G5 CFP-bid race, and 2025 CFP aftermath reporting. League mechanics: 14 teams, divisions gloriously intact — East and West winners meet in the Sun Belt Championship. New this year: Louisiana Tech paid a premium to escape Conference USA a year early and joins the West; Texas State left for the rebuilt Pac-12. And a birthday: Sun Belt football turns 25 this season.*

## Setting the Table: What Happened in 2025 (60-Second Recap)

James Madison delivered the Sun Belt's greatest season — and then the sport delivered its invoice. The Dukes went 12-1 in the regular calendar (9-0 in league play), trailed Old Dominion 27-21 deep into the first half of their showdown and answered with a 42-0 run, pulled away late from Troy in the title game, and grabbed the CFP's final auto-bid spot when five-loss Duke won the ACC and knocked Virginia out of the champions' pool. For one December weekend, the Sun Belt AND the American were both in the 12-team field — a G5 first. Then Oregon hung 51 on JMU in Eugene (a 51-34 loss the Dukes at least made interesting, unlike Tulane's 41-10 evaporation), the "do they belong" discourse raged, and the looting commenced: Bob Chesney left for UCLA with the roster's core, and only four of JMU's 27 players with 300+ snaps return.

The West gave us the sport's cleanest natural experiment: Charles Huff left 2024 champion Marshall for 1-11 Southern Miss and took the roster with him — Marshall's SP+ rating fell 7.6 points and its win total halved, while USM's rose 7.1 points and jumped to 7-6. The Golden Eagles even led the division race before fading late; defense-first Troy took the West at 8-6 and scared JMU for three and a half quarters in the title game. And then Huff did it AGAIN — leaving for Memphis and dropping Southern Miss to dead last nationally in returning production, the same crater he left in Huntington twelve months earlier.

Elsewhere: Old Dominion won 10 for the first time in nine years behind a team that walloped Virginia Tech 45-26 and went 10-1 against everyone not named Indiana or James Madison; Texas State went 7-6 in its Sun Belt farewell; and the bottom of both divisions (Georgia State 1-11, ULM's eight-game skid, South Alabama's post-plunder 4-8) reminded everyone that the Fun Belt's fun is unevenly distributed.

**The 2025 ledger — East:** JMU 12-2 (9-0, champs, CFP), ODU 10-3 (6-2), Georgia Southern 7-6 (4-4), Coastal Carolina 6-7 (5-3), Marshall 5-7 (3-5), App State 5-8 (2-6), Georgia State 1-11 (0-8). **West:** Troy 8-6 (6-3, division champs), Southern Miss 7-6 (5-3), Arkansas State 7-6 (5-3), Texas State 7-6 (3-5, departed), Louisiana 6-7 (5-3), South Alabama 4-8 (3-5), ULM 3-9 (1-7).

**What's new for 2026:**
- **BILLY NAPIER COACHES JAMES MADISON.** Read that again. Fired at Florida (22-23), replaced there by Tulane's Jon Sumrall, Napier returns to the league he once ruled — 34-5 at Louisiana from 2019-21 with two titles — at what Connelly calls maybe the Sun Belt's most well-resourced program. The hire lineage in Harrisonburg is absurd: Cignetti (won a natty at Indiana) → Chesney (UCLA) → the guy Florida just fired, who also happens to be the most accomplished Sun Belt coach of the decade. Underwhelming and overqualified at the same time.
- **The Huff crater, part two:** Southern Miss enters 2026 with the worst returning production in the country (22%, 138th) and a Blake Anderson hire Connelly openly disliked. Marshall, one year further into its own Huff recovery, is quietly a sneaky East contender.
- **Coastal fired a coach DURING a bowl season** — Tim Beck, mid-third-straight-bowl-year, because the standard was slipping (five straight years of decline from the 11-1 peak). Missouri State's Ryan Beard arrives with 12 Bears.
- **Louisiana Tech makes it a three-Louisiana league** — Cumbie's Bulldogs, off their best season in seven years, paid CUSA to leave early, finally giving Louisiana and ULM the in-conference rival they've clamored for. Connelly's exact framing: the hostility quotient just went up. Hell yeah.

## The Playoff Picture (a.k.a. The Résumé Problem)

- **The math is harder this year.** The CFP's fifth spot goes to the highest-ranked G5/G6 champion, and JMU at +1300 sits behind Boise State (+194) and the American duo. SP+ gives the Dukes just an 8.2% shot at 11+ wins — and last year's blueprint required exactly 11 regular-season wins plus chaos above them.
- **The scheduling own-goal:** JMU plays no Power 4 team in 2026, and the committee demonstrably weighs that — Tulane out-ranked JMU last December largely on two P4 scalps. A 12-1 JMU with zero P4 wins versus an 11-2 Boise or a Pac-12 champ with real scalps is a genuinely uncomfortable committee Tuesday.
- **The scar tissue:** JMU's 51-34 Eugene exit was at least competitive (Tulane's was not), and Connelly's data point for the access debate cuts the other way here — ODU tested eventual champion Indiana harder than most of the Big Ten did. The league's depth is real; its December branding is the fight.
- **The honest read:** this is probably a build-back year for the bid. The Sun Belt slid to third in the G5 SP+ average last season and is projected FOURTH of six mid-major leagues now — closer to CUSA than to the American — after losing Cignetti, Sumrall, Chesney, and Huff (twice) in three cycles. The Napier hire is, explicitly, the league's bet on reversing that slide.

# TIER 1 — THE STANDARD-BEARER

## James Madison Dukes

**2025:** 12-2 (9-0), Sun Belt champions, CFP No. 12 seed — lone regular-season loss at Louisville, then the 51-34 Eugene exit. **2026 coach:** Billy Napier, first year. **SP+:** #75, 8.5 avg wins on the 132nd-ranked schedule. **Vegas:** +230 league favorite, 8.5 total (over -130), +1300 for the bid.

**1) The roster.** The teardown is total: of 27 Dukes with 300+ snaps, four return — center Zach Greenberg, WR Braeden Wisloski, edge Amar Thomas, LB Gannon Weathersby. Napier's 41-transfer answer is the most interesting class-construction story in the G5, split between his trademark mass (an O-line haul listed at 6-5/339, 6-8/321, 6-8/310, 6-7/307, 6-6/325) and a deliberate youth strategy Connelly highlights: he vacuumed up the best small-school UNDERCLASSMEN in America — RB Nick Herman (1,034 yards at 7.2 a carry as a Drake freshman), WRs Noah Grevious (VMI) and Jeremiah Harrison (ETSU), DT Kevin Roberts (a Connelly favorite: 18 TFLs at 291 pounds for West Florida), Holy Cross and West Alabama linebackers, a Fordham/Findlay/Shepherd DB trio — plus FBS sophomores with pedigree (LSU blue-chip WR Kylan Billiot, Wisconsin DE Ernest Willor Jr., K-State and Florida linemen). Whoever stars in 2026 has 2027 eligibility; this is a two-year build wearing a one-year favorite's crown. The QB derby is four deep: Memphis's Arrington Maiden, UCF's Davi Belfort, holdover Camden Coleman (13-1 as Richmond's starter), and big sophomore JC Evans.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: Napier ball — size, physicality, evaluation-and-development, the formula that went 34-5 at Louisiana. Strengths: resources, the league's softest schedule (132nd SOS), a proven Sun Belt-domination operating manual, and George Pettaway steadying the backfield. Weaknesses: total unfamiliarity (Connelly's warning: 2026 JMU will look NOTHING like 2025 JMU), a four-way QB derby, and the résumé trap — no P4 opponent means even 12-1 might not out-rank Boise. Goals: win the East, win the title, and force the committee's hand for a third straight Harrisonburg December.

**3) Prediction.** 10-2 and a repeat — the Sept. 26 trip to ODU and the Oct. 29 Troy rematch are the only real land mines on that schedule, and Napier's floor-raising is exactly what a transition roster needs. But the CFP bid slips away on résumé math, and the real story becomes 2027: if the sophomore class hits, JMU opens next season as a top-40 program with continuity nobody in the G5 can match. For your show: the Napier redemption arc versus the Florida subplot writes itself weekly.

# TIER 2 — THE CHALLENGERS

## Old Dominion Monarchs (East)

**2025:** 10-3 (6-2) — Connelly's pick for the best mid-major coaching job in America: from 5-7 and 95th to 10-3 and 50th, a 45-26 walloping of Virginia Tech, and 10-1 against everyone except Indiana and JMU. **SP+:** #89, 7.4 avg wins.

**1) The roster.** The engine left — QB Colton Joseph to Wisconsin, OC Kevin Decker to Memphis, and basically 10.5 offensive starters out the door, with only RB Devin Roche (5-7, 172, and an absurd 3.7 yards after contact) back among the seven 400-yard scrimmage players. The defense is the inversion: coordinator Blake Seiler returns, 10 of 18 regulars are back including sophomore studs Chris Forbes and Jackson Forrest, six of the top nine DBs, and 2024 linebacker stars Mario Thompson and Koa Naotala return from injury. This could be the league's most proven unit. The offensive bet is Ricky Rahne's talent-ID track record: sophomore Quinn Henicle (who won the bowl game over USF with his legs) versus Washington State's Jaxon Potter at QB, with small-school skill flyers (West Liberty's Hunter Patterson, Lenoir-Rhyne's Zion Agnew, Mercer's Kendall Harris) and a deliberately juco-heavy depth strategy Connelly flags as intriguing counter-programming.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: big-play hunting on offense (7th in yards per successful play last year), turnover-forcing aggression on defense (25 takeaways, 13th vs. the dropback). Strengths: the defense, the coach, the culture of a 10-win breakthrough. Weaknesses: an offense with one proven player, and the memory that both 2025 losses that mattered came against the two best teams they played. Goals: host JMU on Sept. 26 with the division on the line in Week 1 of league play — the tone-setter Connelly ranks as the conference's best game.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 — the defense keeps every game close, the offense finds its Joseph impression by mid-October, and the Sept. 26 result decides whether this is a division race or a coronation. If Henicle pops, revise up; Rahne has earned the benefit of every doubt.

## Marshall Thundering Herd (East)

**2025:** 5-7 — the forgivable disaster: dead last nationally in returning production post-Huff, early chaos, then a real October (upset ODU 48-34, outlasted Texas State in double OT) and two brutally unlucky late losses (outgained App State and Georgia Southern 6.6 to 5.1 yards per play, lost by seven combined). **SP+:** #92, 7.8 avg wins — the model's sneaky East pick.

**1) The roster & identity.** By Sun Belt standards this is now a CONTINUITY team: QB Carlos Del Rio-Wilson returns off 2,043 passing and 714 rushing yards, with three of his top four receivers and two line starters, in Rod Smith's dual-threat-QB system that's made a career of exactly this profile. The defense is the rebuild — Tony Gibson brought back Brad Lambert (architect of Marshall's 2019-20 defensive surge) as coordinator, but only seven of 21 regulars return; OLB Javae Gilmore (15 run stops) and safety Daytione Smith are the keepers, and transfer corners Charles McCartherens and Brayden Hall are the most proven imports.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Strengths: the QB, offensive continuity (54th projected offense), and last year's underlying metrics screaming positive regression. Weaknesses: a 119th-ranked defense being handed to a nostalgia hire, and a projection (7.8 wins) that quietly assumes the unlucky bounces flip. Goals: crash the JMU-ODU party — the Oct. 3 trip to Harrisonburg and Oct. 31 trip to ODU are both on Connelly's best-games list.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 and the East's chaos agent — Del Rio-Wilson is the division's best returning QB, and if Lambert gets the defense to merely 90th, Marshall beats one of the two favorites in a game that decides the division. The 2024 champs are the league's best value narrative.

## Troy Trojans (West)

**2025:** 8-6 — West champions and near-spoilers, trailing JMU only 17-14 with five minutes left in the title game. **SP+:** #90, 7.3 avg wins. Parker Year 3.

**1) The roster & identity.** The formula was elite red-zone defense and just-enough offense; now the units swap burdens. Thirteen of 19 defensive regulars are gone — edge Donnie Smith (11 sacks, arguably the league's best returning defender) leads a senior core (Kam Curry, Luke Hodge, TJ Thompson, Justin Powe) over a thin two-deep. The offense inherits the optimism: BOTH quarterbacks return (Goose Crowder and Tucker Kilcrease, 20 combined starts and 3,962 career yards), six experienced linemen plus all-conference transfers Jordon Jones and Luke Petit, and a genuinely exciting OC hire in Adam Austin from Tarleton State's FCS fireworks show. The receiver room is the void — returning wideouts caught SEVEN passes last year, all by Mojo Dortch.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Strengths: QB experience, line depth, a coach who just proved he can win here, and the program's ignition history (Neal Brown 31-8, Sumrall 23-5). Weaknesses: total receiver anonymity and a defense replacing its identity. Goals: become the first West repeat since the division's Louisiana-Troy duopoly days — the Oct. 29 Harrisonburg rematch and Nov. 7 trip to Louisiana Tech are the milestones.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 and right in the four-way West knot — Austin's offense makes them more fun and slightly less sound, and the division comes down to tiebreakers among teams separated by 0.4 projected wins. Parker's arrow is the league's most improved.

## Louisiana Ragin' Cajuns (West)

**2025:** 6-7 — the anti-portal bet failed (seven transfers in, offense fell 45 SP+ spots, Walker Howard busted), salvaged only by a late four-game surge with Lunch Winfield at QB. **SP+:** #104 overall but 7.7 avg wins — the schedule-adjusted West favorite. Desormeaux Year 5, and the league's most fascinating hot seat.

**1) The roster & identity.** The double-down: FIVE transfers this time, the fewest in the league, because Desormeaux believes the pipeline — and the projections half-agree, since Louisiana has the league's third-best returning production and its most interesting sophomore core (Winfield, deep threat Shelton Sampson Jr., C Cooper Fordham, S Kody Jackson, CB Brent Gordon Jr.). Winfield's flashes were real: 14-of-15 for 232 against South Alabama, 131 rushing yards against Arkansas State. Six linemen with starts return, RB transfers Anthony Reagan Jr. and sophomore Steven Blanco restock the ground game, and the identity — physical runs, Winfield keepers, bombs to Sampson — writes itself. The flaw: a front six replacing six of its top eight with essentially no reinforcements, in a league where Louisiana played five 62-point track meets last year.

**2) The seat, full treatment.** Three losing seasons in four (all 6-7) after inheriting Napier's machine; the anti-portal philosophy is either a conviction or an alibi, and 2026 rules on which. **How he escapes:** Winfield leaps, the sophomores hit, 8-4 and a division title shot — genuinely live given the 7.7 projection. **How it ends:** another 6-7 built on a 113th-ranked defense, and the school that watched Napier go 34-5 decides conviction isn't results. **Prediction:** 7-5, division race until mid-November, and the philosophy debate rages either way. The highest-variance bet in the league.

## Louisiana Tech Bulldogs (West — Welcome to the Fun Belt)

**2025 (CUSA):** 8-5 — Cumbie's breakthrough after four straight losing years: best win total and SP+ (77th) in seven seasons, with both units finally good simultaneously. **SP+:** #99, 6.4 avg wins. Best returning production in the league (56%, 53rd nationally).

**1) The roster & identity.** QB Blake Baker was excellent before an ACL tear (backup Trey Kukuk closed the year with three straight wins on his legs) and should be back for the debut. Sure-handed TE Eli Finley returns; the receiver reload runs through healthy-again Jay Wilkerson (20.2 per catch in 2024), Oklahoma State's Talyn Shettron, and return ace Dedrick Latulas; five linemen with starts protect it all; and new OC Nathan Young leans pass. The defense is the identity: Cumbie has nailed back-to-back coordinator hires, and Luke Olson's unit stuffed 38% of plays for zero-or-negative yards (6th in FBS), forced 25 turnovers (9th), and locked the red zone (48.7% TD rate, 11th). LBs Sifa Leota and Jadon Mayfield and ends Emmanuel Oguns and Donovan Rieman keep the front six real; the secondary's total makeover (top five gone) is the one soft spot.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals & the vibes.** Strengths: continuity, defense, a healthy QB, and house-money energy — they PAID to get here early. Weaknesses: secondary, RB proof (NAIA transfer Jaden Miller leaps three levels), and a schedule the league office did not make gentle. Goals: contend immediately — Nov. 7 vs. Troy at home is Connelly's lone West entry on the best-games list, and it's set up as the stakes-setter.

**3) Prediction.** 7-5 in the debut with a signature home November — and the instant-rivalry content (Louisiana and ULM finally get their third sibling) is a gift to the whole league. The hostility quotient, indeed.

## Arkansas State Red Wolves (West)

**2025:** 7-6 — the definitively decent era: 21-18 since 2023. **SP+:** #101, 6.9 avg wins. Butch Jones Year 6, and the schedule handed him a gift: ASU misses the East's top two entirely.

**The gist.** Jaylen Raynor (8,694 career yards) left for Iowa State, so Jones signed FOUR quarterbacks — Texas blue-chipper Trey Owens plus two Vanderbilt transfers (Drew Dickey, Jérémy St-Hilaire) who followed new OC Garrett Altman from Nashville — to battle holdover Ethan Crawford. The supporting cast is the league's most experienced: five of seven line starters back (the Saxton-Myers-Smith interior trio graded elite), both top RBs (Kenyon Clay, Devin Spencer), and five of seven 25-catch receivers. The defense got a 17-transfer makeover aimed at the interior line (MTSU's Shakai Woods, Maine's 290-pound, 10-TFL Elias Sherman) in front of a returning safety corps. The math problem: ASU was 4-1 when allowing under 27 points — and did it five times. **Prediction:** 8-4 and the division's tiebreaker menace; if any of the four QBs is simply good, the schedule makes Jonesboro the sneaky path to the title game. This is the opportunity Year 6 records are judged by.

# TIER 3 — A COUPLE OF BREAKS AWAY (CONNELLY'S LABEL)

## Georgia Southern Eagles (East)
**2025:** 7-6 — four straight bowls under Clay Helton is real fort-holding, but the cracks showed: four one-score wins to scrape eligibility and an 0-4, 45-to-13 average wipeout against top-70 teams. **SP+:** #103, 5.8 wins. The 2026 story is two derbies: Max Johnson's final act (6,356 career yards at LSU/UNC, back from the 2024 leg injury) versus sophomores Turner Helton — yes, Clay's son — and Weston Bryan for the QB job, and new DC Mike Mutz (fresh off a dominant Stephen F. Austin defense) trying to lift a unit that fell to 130th despite ten returning sophomore/junior contributors. Aplin's offense has averaged 59.8 in SP+ for four years, so the floor is real; the receiver/line proof is not (top returnee started twice). **Prediction:** 6-6 — the defense improves, the offense regresses, and the Johnson-vs-Helton derby is a weekly subplot either way. JC French's departure to Cincinnati, by the way, is your AAC-doc crossover.

## Appalachian State Mountaineers (East)
**2025:** 5-8 — the worst season of App's FBS life, completing a slide Connelly charts in brutal eras: .811 ball from 2018-21 to .417 since 2024. **SP+:** #111, 6.1 wins. Loggains' Year 2 is a full teardown: 40 out, 36 in, and only LB Colton Phares and G Cayden Sweatt started more than seven games last year. The reload: Kendal Briles protégé Mike Anthony installs tempo-spread, with Purdue's Malachi Singleton (7.9 per dropback, 8.9 per carry — garbage-time inflated but toolsy) battling UCLA's Henry Hasselbeck at QB, and a Connelly-approved batch of developmental small-schoolers (Towson's Aaron Clark, Findlay's 15.5-TFL hybrid Kevin Henderson, Dayton's Josh Williams) seeding the defense. **Prediction:** 6-6 — the culture equity is real, the talent floor is real, and merely reaching a bowl would mark the refresh as working. Boone deserves the bounce.

## South Alabama Jaguars (West)
**2025:** 4-8 — the poster child for Connelly's "do fun things, get plucked apart" Sun Belt lament: the 2024 breakout shipped Gio Lopez to UNC (another Belichick bridge), Fluff Bothwell to Mississippi State, and more, and the 2025 team fell to its worst ranking since 2019. **SP+:** #115, 5.3 wins. Applewhite's counter-strategy is stark: 27 transfers OUT, only ELEVEN in, betting on returnees and nine jucos. QB Bishop Davenport is more proven than most league starters, corner Jayvon Henderson and OLB Tirrell Johnson anchor the defense, and sophomore LB Caleb Dozier is a breakout pick if the foot injury cooperates. **Prediction:** 5-7 — the admirable-risk roster strategy needs a year of unearthing more Lopezes, and the two-deep holes are just too many. But respect the swing.

## Coastal Carolina Chanticleers (East)
**2025:** 6-7 — bowl-eligible and fired anyway, because five consecutive years of decline from 11-1/17th-in-SP+ is a trend, not a blip (five winless top-50 games at an average of 48.6 to 6.2 is the damning stat). **2026:** Ryan Beard imports Missouri State's standard — 41 transfers, 12 Bears (QB Deuce Bailey, WR Tristian Gardner, a D-line trio), retained explosives (Dominic Knicely, Ibrahim Diawara, Tray Brown), and a four-way QB derby with Arkansas's Trever Jackson and freshman super-athlete Osiris Lopez in the mix. **SP+:** #119, 5.2 wins. **Prediction:** 5-7 with real passing-game fun — Connelly sees potential but won't trust a five-year regression, and neither should you. Yet.

# TIER 4 — JUST LOOKING FOR A PATH TO 6-6

## Southern Miss Golden Eagles (West)
**2025:** 7-6 — the great Huff heist worked, then Huff left for Memphis and took it all back. **2026:** the WORST returning production in the country (22%, 138th): ONE returning offensive starter with more than two starts, ONE returning defender with more than 40 snaps. Even by 2026 standards, that's obliteration. New coach Blake Anderson — a hire Connelly bluntly disliked — rebuilds with promising sophomores (UMass RB Brandon Hood, Wingate's 17.8-per-catch WR Jaylen Himes) and blue-chip flotsam (Ole Miss DE Jeffery Rush, A&M S Jordan Pride), with a three-way QB derby (Lyddy/White/Ethan Hampton). **SP+:** #131, 2.8 wins. **Prediction:** 3-9 — the second consecutive program to eat a Huff departure whole, and the cautionary tale of the caravan era.

## ULM Warhawks (West)
**2025:** 3-9 — a 3-1 start, then eight straight losses as the line leaked and the injuries mounted; Ahmad Hardy's post-transfer stardom at Missouri (before his shooting injury this May — handle with care if it comes up) salts the wound. **SP+:** #132, 2.6 wins. The 2026 kernels: QB Aidan Armenta returns with three of his top four targets, ex-Cane Don Chaney Jr. and D-II star Bryson Roland rebuild the backfield, LB Noah Flemmings is a genuine stud, and six secondary starters return. The line, on both sides, is the sieve-or-survive variable. **Prediction:** 4-8 — modest, real improvement, and Vincent's seat conversation waits for November. Plus: La Tech finally on the schedule as a proper rival. Hostility quotient.

# RAPID-FIRE PODCAST AMMO

**Five dates to circle on air (Connelly's own best-games list, East-heavy for a reason):**
1. **Sept. 26 — James Madison at Old Dominion.** Both teams' league opener. The 27-21-then-42-0 memory. The whole East, possibly settled in September.
2. **Oct. 3 — Marshall at James Madison.** The other East contender walks into Harrisonburg one week later. Napier's roster gets no runway.
3. **Oct. 29 — Troy at James Madison.** The title-game rematch (17-14 with five minutes left, remember), with the champ as the league's designated hinge team.
4. **Oct. 31 — Marshall at Old Dominion.** The 48-34 revenge date; probably an elimination game for the second East ticket.
5. **Nov. 7 — Troy at Louisiana Tech.** The lone West headliner: the debutante's statement chance and the West's stakes-setter.

**Pettiness index (ranked):**
1. Charles Huff leaving Southern Miss for Memphis one year after leaving Marshall for Southern Miss — dropping BOTH exes to dead last (or nearly) in returning production in consecutive years. The caravan takes no prisoners and leaves no starters.
2. Florida firing Billy Napier, hiring the Sun Belt's best current coach (Sumrall), and then watching Napier take over the Sun Belt's best program. Gainesville-Harrisonburg is now a real axis of spite.
3. Coastal Carolina firing Tim Beck DURING bowl prep of a third straight bowl season. Cold-blooded, arguably correct, absolutely pettiness-adjacent.
4. Louisiana Tech paying Conference USA extra money to leave a year early — buying its way into the Fun Belt specifically to fight its neighbors. Connelly's "hostility quotient" line is your segment title.
5. Old Dominion losing its QB to Wisconsin and its OC to Memphis in the same winter — the mid-major tax collected twice from one 10-win team.

**Stat quirks for air:**
- Southern Miss returns ONE defender who played more than 40 snaps. One.
- JMU is projected to fall ~50 SP+ spots... and still win the league by nearly a full projected conference game.
- The West's top four are separated by 0.4 projected conference wins, one year after four teams finished within a game of the division lead. The Sun Belt's parity is a renewable resource.
- ODU went 10-1 against everyone not named Indiana or James Madison — and beat Virginia Tech by 19.
- Marshall outgained App State and Georgia Southern by 1.5 yards per play combined and lost both games by a total of seven points. Regression is coming, in the good direction.
- Sun Belt football is 25 years old this season — and North Texas, now the AAC's cautionary tale, won the first FOUR titles. Connelly's kicker: the title used to go through Denton; now it goes through Harrisonburg.
