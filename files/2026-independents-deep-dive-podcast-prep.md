# 2026 Independents Deep Dive — Notre Dame & UConn — Podcast Prep

Sources synthesized: Athlon, CFN, and SI season previews, ESPN/Connelly's SP+ projections and preview series (UConn was formally covered inside Connelly's American Conference preview), current Vegas numbers, and this week's media-tour coverage. FBS is down to exactly two independents — the sport's biggest brand and its most portable roster — and they could not be entering 2026 from more opposite directions.*

## Setting the Table: Two Programs, Two Universes

Notre Dame enters 2026 as a co-headliner of the national title race after the most controversial exclusion of the playoff era. UConn enters it as the answer to a trivia question — the only non-Notre Dame independent left — after getting strip-mined by the same caravan economy that hit half the Group of 6. One is favored in every game it plays; the other is a chemistry experiment with five returning starters. They share exactly one thing: nobody plays a league schedule, so every week is a résumé argument.

---

# NOTRE DAME FIGHTING IRISH

**2025:** 10-2 — an 0-2 start by a combined FOUR points, then ten straight wins... and the highest-ranked team left out of the College Football Playoff, squeezed by the champions' auto-bid math the same weekend five-loss Duke's ACC title knocked the last at-large domino loose. The Irish then declined bowl season entirely — they haven't played since Thanksgiving weekend, and they've been marinating in it ever since. **2026:** Freeman Year 5. **Vegas:** +700 to win it all (second only to Ohio State's +600), an 11.5 win total that ties Texas Tech for the nation's highest, and — this is the stat for air — favored in EVERY game on the schedule, laying 21+ in each of the first six weeks.

**1) The roster.** The rarest thing in modern college football: continuity at the top of a blue blood. All three coordinators return (Mike Denbrock, Chris Ash, Marty Biagi), and so does the QB — CJ Carr, off a debut of 2,741 yards with a 24-to-6 TD-to-INT ratio, now openly discussed as a Heisman candidate and the face of the program with Jeremiyah Love and Jadarian Price off to the NFL. The receiver room is the deepest of the Freeman era: Jordan Faison (2025 leading receiver), Jaden Greathouse (the title-game star of January 2025), Micah Gilbert, and — the eyebrow-raiser — Ohio State transfers Mylan Graham and Quincy Porter. Rising tackles Guerby Lambert and Will Black bracket a line with veteran Anthonie Knapp sliding inside to guard. Ash's defense (313 yards and 17.6 points allowed per game in Year 1) adds transfer beef inside — Pitt's Francis Brewu and Oregon's 6-5, 330-pound Tionne Gray — around Boubacar Traore, a preseason-acclaim edge, and the poetic return of Keon Keeley, the former ND commit who detoured through Alabama. Freeman's one public flag: a secondary he's called thin, leaning on nickel Dallas Golden and the mercurial Christian Gray. And the fix for the sport's funniest recurring bug: after Notre Dame made ZERO field goals over the final six games of 2025 (Noah Burnette's hip injury broke the position so badly Freeman simply stopped attempting kicks, warping his fourth-down aggression), Purdue's Spencer Porath (15-of-17 last year) arrives, alongside a new Australian punter, Jasper Scaife. Watch list: Carr, Traore, Graham, and — genuinely — the kicker.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: the revenge tour, institutionalized. The team motto is "Leave No Doubt," born from the selection-show snub, and Freeman has been explicit on the media circuit that the program rewatches the pain on purpose — keeping the sting close and using it as fuel rather than blaming the committee. Strengths: QB continuity, coordinator continuity, two-deep talent, the softest opening runway in the sport, and the largest chip-on-shoulder-per-blue-blood ratio ever measured. Weaknesses: the secondary, the schedule's back-loaded trap potential, and the Freeman-as-favorite history — the Marshall, Stanford, and NIU upsets all came as multi-TD favorites, and 2026 makes him the hunted in every single stadium. Season goals: nothing short of ending the 38-year national title drought — every preview from Athlon to CFN uses championship-or-bust language, and the program isn't arguing.

**3) Prediction.** 11-1, a top-4 seed, and the deep January run — with the one loss coming from the exact three-week stretch your show cares about most: at BYU Oct. 17 (a genuine bear trap in Provo, roughly a 10-point line), Navy at Gillette Stadium Oct. 31, and then **Miami, Nov. 7** — the mammoth Canes-Irish collision, in November this time, likely with CFP seeding and Heisman positioning both on the table. For your audience, that's the single most important nonconference date on the 2026 calendar: Carr versus Mensah, the snub-revenge tour versus the title-game-revenge tour, two programs that both spent January watching someone else celebrate. Start the countdown clock now.

# UCONN HUSKIES

**2025:** 9-4 as an independent — the second straight strong year under Jim Mora, who then left for Colorado State and took 16 players with him, among 44 total portal exits. **2026 coach:** Jason Candle, first year — the longtime Toledo boss, who answered the looting by conducting his own: 56 incoming transfers, 19 of them Rockets. The roster math is the whole story, and Connelly's phrasing from the American preview is the definitive version: UConn got North Texas'd, so it North Texas'd Toledo right back. The Huskies enter 2026 with five players who started a game for UConn last year — and eight who started games for Toledo. **SP+:** #107, 5.2 avg wins.

**1) The roster.** A Toledo-flavored rebuild with real pieces: the backfield stacks veterans Kenji Christian (Toledo), Trey Cornist, and Cyncir Bowers (West Virginia); the QB derby pits Toledo's Kalieb Osborne against Tennessee transfer Jake Merklinger, with returning sophomore Tucker McDonald lurking after a good spring; Marshall tackle Christian Richter anchors the line; and Youngstown State's gloriously named Flash Wilson leads the receiver imports. The defensive front six is the strength and it's legitimate — LB K'Von Sherman arrives off a 17-TFL season at Toledo, with end Anas Luqman and tackle Esean Carter combining for 14.5 more, plus Duke corner Moussa Kane behind them. The secondary depth is the athleticism-over-proof gamble Connelly flags as the real risk.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals & the shame of the timing.** Candle's Toledo teams were perennially organized, physical, and QB-stable — the profile travels. Strengths: front-six production, backfield depth, a coach with two decades of MAC winning. Weaknesses: total chemistry unknowns, secondary proof, and no conference title to anchor the season's meaning. The cruelest part is the schedule: this transition year wastes the best home slate in program history — Maryland, Syracuse, and Belichick's North Carolina all visit East Hartford, along with the Sun Belt's two best teams, James Madison and Old Dominion. That's five nationally relevant home dates for a mystery team. Season goals: bowl eligibility, a signature Rentschler upset, and — the subplot humming underneath — auditioning for the American Conference expansion conversation, which is exactly why Connelly folded them into that preview in the first place.

**3) Prediction.** 5-7, dangerous at home, with one genuine scalp — JMU and ODU both have to visit a team with nothing to lose and a Buck-Buchanan-adjacent pass rush. If the Osborne/Merklinger derby produces even league-average QB play, revise to 6-6 and a bowl. Either way, the 2027 question (independence versus the American) becomes the louder story by November.

# RAPID-FIRE PODCAST AMMO

3. **Nov. 7 — Miami vs. Notre Dame.** The one. Both fan bases have circled it since January.
4. **UConn's home gauntlet** — Maryland, Syracuse, UNC, JMU, ODU all at the Rent. Belichick-in-East-Hartford alone is a segment.

**Pettiness index:**
1. Notre Dame skipping bowl season entirely after the snub — the sport's most expensive silent treatment, weaponized into a team motto ("Leave No Doubt").
2. The committee taking ND's spot via the champions' rule the same weekend everyone argued JMU and Tulane didn't belong — then both G5 teams losing by a combined 48. Every ND fan has this reply saved.
3. Keon Keeley committing to Notre Dame, signing with Alabama, and transferring to Notre Dame anyway. The recruiting circle of life.
4. UConn and Toledo effectively swapping identities twelve months apart — Mora loots UConn for CSU, Candle loots Toledo for UConn, and Toledo restocks with Mercer's entire defense (your G6 doc's MAC section). Three dominoes, one caravan.

**Stat quirks:**
- Notre Dame is favored in all 12 games and laying 21+ in each of the first six — and its coach has famously lost to Marshall, Stanford, and NIU as a big favorite. Variance never sleeps.
- The Irish went their final six 2025 games without a made field goal, and the head coach simply stopped calling for kicks. A Purdue transfer is now among the most important players on a title favorite.
- CJ Carr's debut ratio: 24 touchdowns, 6 picks — elite protection numbers for a first-year starter carrying Heisman expectations into Year 2.
- UConn returns five of its own starters and eight of Toledo's. That sentence should be framed.
