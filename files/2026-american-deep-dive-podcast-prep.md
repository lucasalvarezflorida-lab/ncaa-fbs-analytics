*Compiled July 19, 2026. Sources synthesized: ESPN/Bill Connelly's American Conference preview + SP+ projections (May 22), College Football News win totals, current FanDuel Group of 5 CFP-bid odds, and 2025 CFP aftermath reporting. ## Setting the Table: What Happened in 2025 (60-Second Recap)

The American got everything it wanted in 2025 — and then got robbed at gunpoint in December. Tulane went 11-3, beat North Texas 34-21 in the conference title game at Yulman, and made the program's first College Football Playoff. North Texas won 12 games with the NATION'S No. 1 offense. Navy won 11. USF and ECU won nine each. Six different teams spent time in the national conversation, and the committee cycled three of them (Tulane, USF, Memphis) through the projected No. 12 seed before Tulane locked it down.

Then the sport extracted its tax. Tulane got blown out 41-10 by Ole Miss in the CFP first round — with Jon Sumrall coaching the game after already accepting the Florida job — and the "do G5 teams belong" discourse went nuclear. And the offseason looting was historic: of the seven teams (counting UConn) that finished top-60 in SP+, FIVE lost their head coaches — Sumrall to Florida, Ryan Silverfield to Arkansas, Alex Golesh to Auburn, Eric Morris to Oklahoma State, Jim Mora to Colorado State — and a sixth, ECU, lost both coordinators. The rosters got strip-mined to match. North Texas's list of returning offensive players with 100+ snaps is, verbatim from Connelly's preview, null.

So 2026 is a stress test of the whole enterprise: can the most consistently excellent Group of 5/6 conference of the last decade reload its top shelf in one offseason, with the rebuilt Pac-12 (and Boise State) now openly hunting the CFP bid the American has treated as its birthright?

**The 2025 ledger:** Tulane 11-3 (8-1, champs, CFP), North Texas 12-2 (7-2), Navy 11-2 (7-1), USF 9-4 (6-2), ECU 9-4 (6-2), Memphis 8-5 (4-4), UTSA 7-6 (4-4), Army 7-6 (4-4), Temple 5-7 (3-5), Rice 5-8 (2-6), FAU 4-8 (3-5), UAB 4-8 (2-6), Tulsa 4-8 (1-7), Charlotte 1-11 (0-8). Honorary: UConn 9-4 as an independent.

**What's new for 2026:**
- **Five new head coaches, each a story:** Will Hall (Tulane — the former Southern Miss head man and old Fritz-era Tulane OC, in a hire ESPN's carousel rankings graded as uninspiring, which is doing a lot of work in the projections below), Charles Huff (Memphis — third stop of the traveling roster caravan: Marshall to Southern Miss to Memphis, with 53 transfers including 17 Golden Eagles), Brian Hartline (USF — the Ohio State OC and former Dolphins receiver, bringing 10 blue-chippers and 24 power-conference transfers to Tampa), Neal Brown (North Texas — the old 31-8 Troy builder, inheriting the null roster), and Jason Candle (UConn — who responded to Mora's exit by, in Connelly's perfect phrasing, North Texas-ing Toledo).
- **The stakes changed shape:** the Pac-12 relaunched with Boise State, and FanDuel prices the Broncos at +194 to make the CFP — miles ahead of American co-favorites Memphis and Tulane at +820. The bid the American held in 2025 now has a bigger, better-branded predator.
- **USF's Raymond James farewell (maybe):** with the on-campus stadium targeted for 2027, the Nov. 27 Tulane-at-USF finale is being framed as perhaps the last Bulls home game at RayJay. Circle it.

## The Playoff Picture (a.k.a. The Bid War)

- **The math:** the CFP's fifth auto-bid goes to the highest-ranked G5/G6 champion. In 2025 the American won that war AND stole a second bid (more below on JMU). In 2026, Boise State (+194) looms over everyone — the books think the bid runs through the Pac-12 now.
- **The résumé problem:** a fractured, six-way title race is great TV and terrible for rankings. If the champ emerges at 10-3 out of a demolition derby, Boise or a one-loss Pac-12 team probably takes the spot. The American needs a runaway — an 11-1 or 12-1 champ — and SP+ gives no team better than USF's 3.2% odds of even reaching 11 wins.
- **The scar tissue:** the league's 2025 CFP showing was a 41-10 first-round blowout, and the discourse afterward was brutal — nationally, the argument was that higher-ranked Notre Dame and BYU deserved the spots. Sumrall's postgame defense was the league's rallying cry: <cite index="15-1">"There should be access for at least one G-5 team moving forward."</cite> Every 2026 American broadcast happens in that shadow.
- **Connelly's counterfactual for your access-debate segment:** under a hypothetical 24-team CFP using penultimate rankings, the American would have earned multiple bids in eight different seasons since 2015 — three in 2015 alone — and been shut out only twice. No mid-major conference would benefit more from expansion.

# TIER 1 — THE CONTENDERS (SIX-CAR PILEUP DIVISION)

## Navy Midshipmen

**2025:** 11-2 (7-1) — 21-5 over two years under Brian Newberry. **SP+:** #63, 8.1 avg wins, 6.6 in conference — the projected favorite. **Vegas:** +1800 for the CFP bid (the value play if you believe the model over the market).

**1) The roster.** The Blake Horvath era ends after 2,549 career non-sack rushing yards, 33 rushing TDs, and — the part that made this offense special — 2,933 passing yards at 16.6 per completion. Drew Cronic's modernized option throws when the numbers say throw. The heir got a real preview: Braxton Woodson has 19 appearances, 6.7 yards per non-sack carry, and 13.1 per completion in relief work. The caution flag is everything around him — Horvath, Eli Heidenreich, Alex Tecza, and Brandon Chatman took 4,257 scrimmage yards with them, and the leading returning skill player caught 136 yards' worth of passes. But three line starters plus two key backups return, and the fullback pipeline never misses. The defense is the quiet story: EIGHT of 15 regulars back (unusual riches for an academy), led by lineman Griffen Willis and LB MarcAnthony Parker, in a scheme that's finished between 53rd and 75th in defensive SP+ every year since Newberry arrived as coordinator.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: aggressive run defense, third-and-long pass rush, and an option offense that punishes light boxes through the air. Strengths: continuity of scheme, defensive experience, and the schedule — Navy plays only two of the six teams projected closest to it in conference, is a projected underdog ONLY against Notre Dame, and is at least a four-point favorite in every American game but one. Weaknesses: zero proven skill depth, and the annual academy variance. Goals: explicitly, Navy's first conference title game appearance in a decade — and the league's flag in the bid war.

**3) Prediction.** 10-2, a title-game berth, and the SP+-vs-Vegas argument settled in the model's favor. The Oct. 17 trip to the Alamodome (somehow the first Navy-UTSA game ever) and the Nov. 21 Memphis visit decide whether it's 11-1 and a real CFP case instead. Also flag Nov. 27 for a piece of poetry: it's the 100th anniversary, nearly to the day, of the 1926 Army-Navy 21-21 tie that christened Soldier Field in front of 110,000 people — the game that made Army-Navy, Army-Navy.

## Memphis Tigers

**2025:** 8-5 in Silverfield's finale. **2026 coach:** Charles Huff, first year — the caravan's third stop. **SP+:** #70, 7.5 avg wins. **Vegas:** +820 CFP-bid co-favorite. CFN: 7.5 total, projected 8-4.

**1) The roster.** Memphis has averaged 8.9 wins a year since 2014 — the steadiest program in the G5 — and it just hired the man who flipped Southern Miss from 1-11 to 7-6 in one season by importing Marshall's roster. Now he's imported Southern Miss's: 53 transfers, 17 Golden Eagles, one of the biggest roster flips in America. The QB derby is the league's most interesting — Air Noland (the former top-60 recruit, on his third school in three years via Ohio State and South Carolina) versus Marcus Stokes (3,297 yards and 30 TDs at Division II West Florida). Connelly's read: one gives you the ceiling, one the floor, and either works. The skill room is well-traveled and semi-proven (Dallan Hayden, Manny Covey, Tychaun Chapman, Terrell Timmons Jr.), the line is one returnee (Parker Mitchell) plus four G6 starters and two FCS vets, and the OC hire is the league's best: Kevin Decker, who called Old Dominion's explosive 2025 offense, poached straight from a division rival's staff. The defense is USM East — J'Mond Tapp (13 TFLs, the most proven defender on the roster), safeties Ahmere and Ian Foster, plus blue-chip flotsam (K.J. Miles, Christian Thatcher, Stonka Burnside) and small-school hitters.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: Huff ball — physicality, roster churn as a weapon, chemistry on fast-forward. Strengths: institutional floor, coaching fit, athletic two-deep, and the market's belief. Weaknesses: 111th in returning production, everything is theoretical until October, and Huff's model has never faced this level of expectation in Year 1. Goals: the title game — and Connelly's structural note makes them the fulcrum either way: Memphis is his named hinge team, with ECU and Army visiting (Oct. 22, Oct. 31) then trips to USF (Nov. 12) and Navy (Nov. 21) forming the league's four-game November tribunal.

**3) Prediction.** 9-3 and the title game via the hinge games, with the Navy trip deciding home field. The Huff pattern says Year 1 chemistry arrives fast; the Vegas co-favorite price says the sharps agree. If Noland finally sticks somewhere, Memphis wins this league.

## UTSA Roadrunners

**2025:** 7-6 — but 2-6 in one-score games over two years, the quiet tax on a good roster. **SP+:** #73, 7.5 avg wins. Traylor Year 7 (53-26).

**1) The roster.** The steadiness play: Owen McCown returns at QB (618 yards and nine TDs in his two-game heater against Tulane and ECU alone), with big-play back Will Henderson III (6.9 per carry, 3.5 after contact), two of the top three receivers (AJ Wilson at 17.0 a catch), and three-plus line starters. Traylor's offenses have averaged 31.8 in offensive SP+ over five years; this one should keep the streak. The defense turns over (8 of 22 regulars back) but that's less scary when you've been outside the top 80 four of five years anyway — coordinator Jess Loepp hunts havoc and lives with big plays. The portal brought edge Elijah Baldwin, a 10-INT safety tandem of Xavier Walton and Nate Robinson Jr., and blue-chip fliers including Collins Acheampong (the 6-6 edge who passed through Miami) and Texas Tech's Dylan Spencer.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: explosive offense, opportunistic defense, and the Alamodome — UTSA overachieved SP+ projections by 15.4 PPG at home and underachieved by 12.3 on the road, the most extreme home-road split in the league. Strengths: QB continuity, coaching stability, home-field. Weaknesses: road performance and one-score luck. Goals: flip the coin flips and host November games that matter.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 with one enormous home upset — Navy's Oct. 17 visit is the obvious candidate, and if UTSA takes it, the title-game math scrambles completely. Traylor's seat is a throne; the only question is who tries to buy it in December.

## East Carolina Pirates

**2025:** 9-4 (6-2) — Harrell is 14-5 after the program went 5-14 in the 19 games before him, with ECU's first top-40 SP+ finish in 25 years. **SP+:** #76, 7.8 avg wins. 

**1) The roster.** The punishment for mid-major success, itemized: both coordinators gone, ten players to power conferences — including QB Katin Houser (Illinois), two all-conference offensive linemen, and safety Ja'Marley Riddle (Georgia). Harrell's response was a masterclass in lateral shopping: he hired North Texas's OC (Jordan Davis, architect of the No. 1 offense) and Memphis's DC (Jordon Hankins), then raided the G5's proven middle class — North Texas's RB Ashton Gray and WR Landon Sides, Marshall's Michael Allen, App State's Kanen Hamlett and Zyeir Gamble, Kennesaw and Southern Miss linemen. The QB battle is the aforementioned Canes special: Mitch Griffis (2,314 career yards, scrambler-gambler streak, via Wake Forest and Texas Tech) versus Emory Williams (the pocket operator with a 70.8 career QBR and a Miami spot-start win on his résumé). Ex-Cane Ray Ray Joseph adds juice out wide.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: whatever Harrell touches turns competent — that's now a two-year data set. Strengths: coaching, schedule (plays only one of the top three projected teams, plays all of the bottom three), and a transfer class built on production rather than pedigree. Weaknesses: 110th in returning production and two first-year coordinators. Goals: prove 2025 was the program, not the roster.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4, agonizingly close to the title game, and Harrell becomes the hottest name on every December list anyway. Connelly's line is the right frame: calling ECU a contender after this much turnover made him nervous, but nothing Harrell has done justifies doubting him yet.

## Army Black Knights

**2025:** 7-6 — with the season explained by one stat: 4-5 in one-score games after going 23-16 in them over the previous eight years. **SP+:** #81, 7.1 avg wins. Monken Year 13. **Vegas:** +1600 for the bid, ahead of Navy — make of that what you will.

**1) The roster.** Cale Hellums returns, and the splits are the whole argument: 2.69 points per drive with him versus 1.73 without, 44.8% third-down conversion versus 29.5%. Four line starters are back including all-conference center Brady Small, fullback Carson Smith leads the room, deep threat Brady Anderson averaged an outrageous 27.2 yards per catch, and sophomore Godspower Nwawuihe announced himself with 171 yards and two scores in the Fenway Bowl. The defense is the concern — the three primary linebackers and five of six DBs are gone, returning linebackers combined for 22 tackles and zero TFLs, and the bend-don't-break scheme depends on exactly that second level. End Jack Bousum and the upperclassman pipeline (Cole Searight, Jaxon Hammond) carry the reload.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Identity: possession warfare with a QB who converts. Strengths: Hellums, the line, thirteen years of Monken systems. Weaknesses: linebacker proof and the close-game gods — TEN one-score games are projected again. Goals: recover the coin-flip magic and crash a title race with Navy for the first time in the modern format.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4, because the close-game pendulum never stays stuck. The Oct. 10 Tulane visit (both teams' conference opener, Tulane off a bye) and Oct. 31 at Memphis are the season. And the Army-Navy centennial subplot — 100 years since Soldier Field — gives December's game a national-feature glow regardless of records.

# TIER 2 — A COUPLE OF BREAKS AWAY (CONNELLY'S OWN LABEL)

## South Florida Bulls

**2025:** 9-4 (6-2) in Golesh's finale — then Auburn took the coach and QB Byrum Brown and eleven Bulls followed him. **2026 coach:** Brian Hartline, first year. **SP+:** #80, 7.9 avg wins on the EASIEST schedule in the country (125th SOS).

**1) The roster.** The bet is talent-mass: 10 former blue-chippers, 24 power-conference transfers, and a two-deep that Connelly says will immediately look the part. QB Michael Van Buren Jr. is the classic reclamation — real promise as a Mississippi State freshman, then caught in LSU's 2025 offensive disaster. Around him: holdovers Thomas Shrader and Mudia Reuben, Dartmouth workhorse D.J. Crowther, and a receiver board of Cameron Seldon (Virginia Tech), Kenny Odom, and Arhmad Branch, anchored by all-Sun Belt guard Caleb Cook. The defense goes to ECU's aggressive Josh Aldridge, with corner Za'Quan Bryan and havoc bets MarShon Oxley and Shamar Meikle — plus the single best transfer note in the league: Norfolk State's Major Dillard, a 330-POUND tackle who made 70 tackles chasing plays sideline to sideline.

**2) The catch.** Connelly's caution is tactical, not physical: Ohio State's most conservative recent offenses were the two with Hartline as coordinator, and OC Tim Beck's recent track record is uninspiring. If USF scores, it'll be because of superior athletes — which, on THIS schedule, might be enough. Strengths: talent, schedule, recruiting momentum, stadium-era energy. Weaknesses: scheme proof, chemistry, and a conference slate that gives them only 4.5 projected league wins despite 7.9 overall (translation: the nonconference is soft and the league draw is not).

**3) Prediction.** 9-3 and dangerous, with the Nov. 12 Memphis visit and the Nov. 27 Tulane finale — perhaps Raymond James's last Bulls game — as the November theater. If Van Buren hits, the Hartline era starts a year ahead of schedule, and the 2027 stadium opens with title expectations. For your show: this is the closest thing your listeners have to a local rooting interest in the race.

## Tulane Green Wave

**2025:** 11-3 (8-1), American champions, CFP — and three losses by a combined 88 points, which is why the computers never believed. **2026 coach:** Will Hall, first year. **SP+:** #87, 6.5 avg wins, projected SEVENTH. CFN: 8-4.

**1) The roster.** The exodus: Sumrall to Florida, the P4 raid on the two-deep, and only about six returning starters. What's left is genuinely fun — RB Jamauri McClure closed 2025 with 490 yards at 6.5 a pop over the last five games, Anthony Brown-Stephens and Zycarl Lewis Jr. return out wide, and FSU's Jaylin Lucas gives Hall a gadget star. The QB derby is wide open: Kadin Semonza, the well-traveled Zeon Chriss-Gremillion, or sophomore Dagan Bruno. The defense keeps LB Chris Rodgers and a sound secondary core (E'zaiah Shine, Jack Tchienchou, USF transfer Kajuan Banks) but lost its top four linemen with only one semi-proven replacement — a real problem for a unit that allowed 127 fourth-quarter points last year (the hidden flaw under the title run).

**2) The frame.** Connelly's regression-monster explanation deserves your airtime: Tulane's projection isn't a talent judgment, it's an infrastructure test. The program was bad for most of its 20-year baseline until this four-year, 43-win supernova — so when SP+ sees massive overachievement plus a coaching change (to a hire the carousel rankings panned), it forecasts gravity. The counter-case: Tulane already survived Fritz-to-Sumrall seamlessly, the December standard is institutional now (a fifth straight title-game trip would come under a THIRD different coach), and the schedule closes with the North Texas rematch at Yulman and the trip to USF.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 — the machine wobbles but doesn't crash, McClure becomes a star, and the Wave play spoiler in the exact November games (UNT Nov. 21, at USF Nov. 27) that decide the title game field. If Hall finds a QB by October, revise upward; the infrastructure argument is stronger than the model thinks.

# TIER 3 — THE RISERS

## Florida Atlantic Owls
**2025:** 4-8 — but the most entertaining 4-8 in the league, and 0-7 vs. top-100 teams tells you the gap. **SP+:** #95, 6.2 wins. The case: the BEST returning production in the Group of 6 (64%, 19th nationally), Caden Veltkamp back from shoulder surgery after finishing eighth nationally with 3,641 passing yards in the country's fastest offense, 1,000-yard slot Easton Messer retained, and a transfer class Connelly flat-out loved — headlined by the small-school haul of the year: DE Kenyon Garner, who posted TWENTY-EIGHT tackles for loss at Division II Livingstone (an Okeechobee kid coming home to Florida), plus disruptors Nate Fischer, Ahlston Ware, and Bo Simpson-Nealy behind 300-pound SEC-adjacent tackles. The catch: the conference schedule stiffens (3.6 projected league wins), and the defense has to jump from 131st to functional. **Prediction:** 7-5 and bowling — Boca gets a winner, Veltkamp flirts with 4,000, and Garner leads the league in TFLs. Your most fun local segment of the year.

## Tulsa Golden Hurricane
**2025:** 4-8 (1-7) — but with a freshman QB (Baylor Hayes, 2,158 yards), a first win over Oklahoma State since 1998, and late upsets of Oregon State and Army. **SP+:** #98, 6.4 wins — second-best returning production in the G6 behind FAU. Five linemen back averaging 6-5, 314; six of the top eight DBs return including five-INT corner Elijah Green; and Tre Lamb restocked the skill room with a running back haul (DJ McKinney, Trequan Jones, Damari Alston, Seth Davis) Connelly says beats most power-conference classes. The math to fix: 30+ points scored three times, 30+ allowed seven times. **Prediction:** 6-6 and a bowl — the Hayes-Year-2 leap plus continuity is the classic G5 riser formula, and the Nov. 7 trip to Tulane suddenly matters.

## Temple Owls
**2025:** 5-7 — a tale of two months: 5-3 and +9.4 PPG versus projections through October, then 0-4 and -12.1 in November when the depth ran out. Still the program's best record and ranking of the 2020s. **SP+:** #102, 5.8 wins. K.C. Keeler's Year 2 blueprint: four returning line starters, a two-headed run game (Hunter Smith 6.5 a carry, Keveun Mason 6.0), TE Peter Clarke, and a blue-blood-backup QB derby — Jaxon Smolik (Penn State) vs. Ajani Sheppard (Washington State), who've combined for 23 career passing yards. The defense (109th) gets the Keeler special: five jucos, eleven transfers, and a track record that says trust the man with nine FCS/D-III title-game appearances. **Prediction:** 6-6 and the program's first bowl since 2019 — November depth is the whole question.

# TIER 4 — THE GREAT RESETS

## North Texas Mean Green
**2025:** 12-2, the NATION'S No. 1 offense, a title-game run — then Eric Morris took half the operation to Stillwater and the rest scattered. The two most brutal lines in Connelly's preview: the full list of returnees with 200+ snaps is one player (DT Terrell Washington), and the list of offensive returnees with 100+ snaps is null. **2026:** Neal Brown — 31-8 in his Troy prime — starts from actual zero, with a fun QB room (Tayven Jackson, Chaston Ditta), WVU's Jahiem White at RB, and proven wanderer Udoka Ezeani (12.5 TFLs at UTEP, his fifth school) headlining the defense. **SP+:** #109, 5.3 wins — an 87-spot projected fall, the largest in the league's history of getting looted. **Prediction:** 5-7 with flashes — and the Nov. 21 return to Yulman for the championship rematch as pure poetry. The 25-year echo for your Sun Belt crossover: North Texas won the first four Sun Belt titles ever contested. Dynasties are rentals now.

**2025:** 9-4 as an independent — then Mora left for Colorado State with 16 players, and UConn responded by hiring Toledo's Jason Candle, who brought 19 Rockets among 56 transfers. Five returning UConn starters, eight returning Toledo starters — Connelly's "did you keep up with all that?" is the only honest summary. **SP+:** #107, 5.2 wins. The home slate is a bummer to waste on a transition year: Maryland, Syracuse, Belichick's UNC, plus JMU and ODU. **Prediction:** 5-7, dangerous at the Rent, and a 2027 American-expansion subplot simmering underneath everything.

# TIER 5 — JUST LOOKING FOR A PATH TO 6-6

## Rice Owls
