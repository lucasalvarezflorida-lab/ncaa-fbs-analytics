## Setting the Table: What Happened in 2025 (60-Second Recap)

Texas Tech happened. The Red Raiders — the roster Joey McGuire and GM James Blanchard built with the biggest checkbook in the conference — outscored their ten Big 12 opponents by an average of 27 points (32 from November on), throttled BYU twice (34-7 in Lubbock, 29-7 in the title game), and won the program's first Big 12 championship at 12-1 before Oregon shut them out in a CFP quarterfinal. Connelly's framing is the story of the league: he spent years joking that every Big 12 game is a three-point coin flip — and now he's openly asking whether Lubbock is building the conference's version of the Clemson dynasty.

Behind them: BYU went 12-2 with true freshman QB Bear Bachmeier and lost only to Tech (twice); Utah won 11 in Kyle Whittingham's farewell, ten by double digits, losing only to Tech and BYU; Arizona (9-4) and Houston (10-3) surged; Arizona State salvaged an injury-wrecked title defense with the league's biggest upset (over Tech); and the bottom fell out in two places — Colorado collapsed to 3-9 in the first post-Shedeur/Hunter year with Deion Sanders battling health problems, and Oklahoma State delivered a 1-11 season Connelly calls the program's worst in more than 60 years, ending the Gundy era.

**The 2025 ledger:** Texas Tech 12-2 (Big 12 champs), BYU 12-2, Utah 11-2, Houston 10-3, Arizona 9-4, TCU 9-4, Iowa State 8-4, Arizona State 8-5, Cincinnati 7-6, Kansas State 6-6, Kansas 5-7, Baylor 5-7, UCF 5-7, West Virginia 4-8, Colorado 3-9, Oklahoma State 1-11.

**What's new for 2026:**
- **The Sorsby saga — your lead segment.** The full timeline: Brendan Sorsby (Cincinnati's QB, the No. 1 quarterback in the portal) signs with Texas Tech in January as the final piece of a title defense. A gambling investigation surfaces — bets tied to his Cincinnati tenure, which Cincinnati denies knowing about. The NCAA rules him ineligible; Sorsby files an injunction seeking 2026 eligibility; Tech appeals as high as it can go and loses; in mid-June, Sorsby declares for the NFL supplemental draft; in July, the NFL says no. He is, in Dan Wetzel's words, done with college sports — no supplemental draft, waiting on 2027, and Cincy's coach is fielding calls from NFL teams anyway. The wreckage: Tech loses its QB without a snap, the appeals fight left scar tissue between Tech, the league office, and member schools, and it's the sport's biggest gambling-eligibility case yet.
- **Four new head coaches:** Morgan Scalley (Utah — the third coach since 1990, promoted after Whittingham's exit to Michigan), Collin Klein (Kansas State — the program legend returning after Chris Klieman's retirement, via two years on Mike Elko's A&M staff), Eric Morris (Oklahoma State, from North Texas — with half of the nation's No. 1 offense in tow), and Jimmy Rogers (Iowa State, from Washington State, after Matt Campbell left for Penn State and 55 players hit the portal).
- **DJ Lagway plays here now.** The former No. 1 QB recruit's chaotic portal tour (FSU visit, Ole Miss visit, wobbling commitment) ended in Waco. Baylor's season — and Dave Aranda's job — rides on him.
- **Passport required:** TCU opens against North Carolina in Dublin (Aug. 29 — yes, Belichick's Tar Heels, a direct bridge to your ACC episode), and Arizona State and Kansas play a conference game at Wembley Stadium on Sept. 19 in the first Union Jack Classic.

## The Playoff Picture

- **SP+ says one lock, one live wire:** Tech's 62.9% at 11+ wins makes them the most probable conference champion in the Power 4 by the numbers, even post-Sorsby. BYU (9.7%) and Utah (6.6%) are the only realistic second bids — and they play each other Nov. 7 in the Holy War, plus BYU hosts Notre Dame in mid-October in one of the biggest nonconference games in program history. A BYU team that survives that gauntlet at 11-1 is a lock at-large; anything less and this is probably a one-bid league.
- **The Tech asterisk:** last year they were 12-1 and got shut out by Oregon in the quarterfinal — the QB limitation exposed in one game. The entire 2026 CFP thesis in Lubbock was "same team + Sorsby." Now it's "same team + a Tulsa transfer or a rehabbing sophomore," which is why the national board has them 8th (+1600) instead of top-5.
- **CBS's read (Crawford):** Arizona 8-4, Iowa State 4-8, BYU around 10-2 — the projections mostly rubber-stamp the SP+ shape with Tech alone at the top.

# TIER 1 — THE BEHEMOTH

## Texas Tech Red Raiders

**2025:** 12-2 (9-1), Big 12 champions, No. 3 in SP+ — beat BYU and Utah three combined times by a 97-24 aggregate, lost only to Arizona State (without their starting QB) and Oregon (shutout, CFP quarterfinal). **2026:** McGuire Year 5. **SP+:** #7, 10.7 avg wins, 62.9% at 11+. **Vegas:** -105 to repeat, +1600 national.

**1) The roster.** Start with what's gone: Sorsby (never played a snap — see the saga above), plus maybe the two best defensive players in America (edge David Bailey: 23 TFLs, 14.5 sacks; LB Jacob Rodriguez and his absurd 16.6% tackle rate), four of six top defensive linemen, and the top two receivers. Now what's left and added, which is why nobody's panicking: the QB room is Kirk Francis (Tulsa transfer, 12 career starts, 3,045 yards — likely the Week 1 starter), Will Hammond (the electric sophomore who nearly beat Utah in relief last September, returning from an October ACL and possibly not ready for the opener), plus redshirt freshman Lloyd Jones III and true freshman Stephen Cannon. The backfield is a three-headed monster: Cameron Dickey and J'Koby Williams (1,992 combined yards, 20 TDs) plus Quinten Joyner, the USC transfer who averaged 7.6 a carry in 2024 before missing 2025 hurt. Three O-line starters return, joined by Louisville guard Jordan Church and five-star freshman tackle Felix Ojo. The receiver reload: Kenny Johnson (Pitt — also an elite returner), Malcolm Simmons (Auburn — whose per-catch and per-route numbers beat Cam Coleman's), Donte Lee Jr., Jalen Jones. The defensive reload is a Blanchard portfolio piece — Connelly literally says he can't pretend to worry: tackles Julien Laventure and Jojo Johnson (21 combined TFLs), edges Adam Trick (a Connelly top-10 transfer) and Trey White (25.5 combined TFLs), linebackers Austin Romaine (K-State) and Corey Platt Jr. (Houston), around returnees Ben Roberts and A.J. Holmes Jr. The secondary — Brice Pollock (five INTs, 11.3 QBR allowed), Amier Boyd, Brenden Jordan, John Curry — might be the league's best. Watch list: the QB timeline, Dickey, Trick, Pollock.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Mack Leftwich's tempo-spread with a bell-cow run game — a touch all-or-nothing last year, now with more proven receivers and less proven quarterbacking. Defense: Shiel Wood's havoc machine, rebuilt with the portal's most disruptive available humans. Strengths: two-deep talent nobody in the league approaches, the No. 2 portal class + No. 19 recruiting class (both league bests), a schedule with no top-90 nonconference opponent and none of the league's next three contenders. Weaknesses: the QB room's combined résumé is one Tulsa season and one great relief outing, and the memory of a playoff shutout when the QB was the ceiling. Also: the political wounds from the Sorsby fight are real, and every league rival is rooting for the collapse. Season goals: repeat, then win a CFP game — the only box left.

**3) Prediction.** 11-1, back to Arlington on the new Friday night stage, and a top-6 seed. The one loss comes on the road somewhere unglamorous (Houston's Week 3 visit to Lubbock is the first real test — flag it). But the January question is unchanged from last year: can this QB room win a playoff game against an elite defense? McGuire's seat is ice; the dynasty question is the only one that matters.

# TIER 2 — THE CHALLENGERS

## BYU Cougars

**2025:** 12-2 (8-2), 23-4 over two years, CCG runner-up — both losses to Tech by a combined 49 points. **SP+:** #18, 8.7 avg wins. **Vegas:** 8.5 total (CBS projected ~10-2), sub-+1000 title odds.

**1) The roster.** Bear Bachmeier returns after a true-freshman season Connelly calls deliciously high-level — the safest QB bet in the conference. LJ Martin (1,300 yards) returns behind four veteran linemen including all-conference center Bruce Mitchell, plus Washington tackle transfer Paki Finau. The flags: the top three pass targets are gone (Martin and USC TE transfer Walker Lyons are the safety valves; Jojo Phillips and Oregon transfer Kyler Kasper are big-bodied potential, nothing more yet), and DC Jay Hill left for Michigan — longtime assistant Kelly Poppinga inherits ten returning rotation defenders, headlined by LB pair Isaiah Glasker and Siale Esera, corners Evan Johnson and Therrian Alexander III, and 6-4 safety Faletau Satuala. Young edges Nusi Taumoepeau and Tausili Akana carry the pressure-rate promise of a sack breakout. Watch list: Bachmeier, Glasker, the WR mystery.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: efficient, physical, QB-run-flavored — potentially BYU's best since the legendary 2020 unit if receivers emerge. Defense: experienced everywhere with a new voice in the booth. Strengths: continuity (177 returning starts, tops in the league), QB, culture, home-field. Weaknesses: pass-catching proof, pass rush conversion, the Hill departure. Season goals: get over the Tech hump they never touched — and win the two biggest home games in program memory: Arizona Sept. 12 and NOTRE DAME in mid-October.

**3) Prediction.** 10-2 and the league's second CFP bid — with the Notre Dame game as a season-defining at-large résumé bomb either way. Connelly's running joke that he's always wrong about BYU is doing a lot of work here; the fundamentals say top-15, and this time there's no Tech on the schedule until Arlington. Sitake's seat: nonexistent; he's built the second-best program in the league.

## Utah Utes

**2025:** 11-2, 8th in SP+ — Whittingham's farewell masterpiece, ten wins by double digits. **2026 coach:** Morgan Scalley, first year — the fourth Utah head coach since 1989, promoted after two decades as the defensive architect. **SP+:** #24, 8.4 avg wins. **Vegas:** 8.5, sub-+1000.

**1) The roster.** The two keepers are enormous: QB Devon Dampier (the dual-threat engine of a No. 6 offense that led the nation in red-zone TD rate) and 900-yard RB Wayshawn Parker. But they're the ONLY offensive returnees with more than five starts, OC Jason Beck followed Whittingham to Michigan, and all five line starters are gone. Scalley's fixes: Utah State OC Kevin McGiven (another Bronco Mendenhall-tree dual-threat schemer) with his top receiver Braden Pegan in tow, Montana State OL transfer Cedric Jefferson, and a senior-heavy backup pipeline. The defense keeps stars (LB Johnathan Hall, nickel Jackson Bennee) but lost its top four linemen and three of four DBs; longtime LB coach Colton Swan takes the coordinator chair, transfers Elijah Reed and James Chenault patch the corners, and the veteran leadership layer (Holtzclaw, Reynolds, Ritchie) carries the culture bet. Watch list: Dampier, Hall, the entire offensive line.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: dual-threat leverage ball under a new-but-familiar schemer. Defense: Scalley's own top-20 machine, now delegated. Strengths: QB, culture continuity, a friendly schedule (misses Tech, hosts BYU). Weaknesses: five new O-linemen, a rebuilt defensive front and secondary, and the succession question — Connelly notes promoted-coordinator defenses don't always stay elite. Season goals: keep the machine humming through the transition, win the Holy War at home Nov. 7, survive the brutal back-to-back at Arizona (Nov. 14) and at TCU (Nov. 21).

**3) Prediction.** 9-3 — the November three-week gauntlet (BYU, at Arizona, at TCU) claims one game a fresh line can't survive, and Utah plays spoiler-kingmaker rather than champion. But the floor here is remarkably high for a Year 1 coach; three coaches in 36 years is the whole story of why.

## Kansas State Wildcats

**2025:** 6-6 — a preseason top-18 team that lost five of seven one-score games and never fixed the run game; Chris Klieman retired. **2026 coach:** Collin Klein, first year — the program-legend QB and former OC, back after two years running Texas A&M's offense. Connelly's word for the hire: it just seems perfect (with the "so did Frost-to-Nebraska" asterisk attached). **SP+:** #29, 8.2 avg wins. **Vegas:** ~+1200 tier.

**1) The roster.** QB Avery Johnson stayed — the whole ballgame. His 2025 was an identity crisis (elite athlete intermittently cosplaying as a pocket passer), and Klein, the human-third-down-conversion himself, is the ideal whisperer. Around him: RBs Joe Jackson and Oklahoma State transfer Rodney Fields Jr., returning targets Jaron Tibbs and TE Garrett Oakley, plus blue-chip WR transfers Josh Manning (Missouri) and Izaiah Williams (A&M). The line is the fear: All-conference LT John Pastore and then six transfers and a juco. The defense, handed to A&M co-coordinator Jordan Peterson, was gutted (six of 19 regulars back) but restocked with Connelly's favorite front-six haul in the league: edges Wendell Gregory (15.5 TFLs on a 1-11 Oklahoma State team) and Elijah Hill, LB Mekhi Mason (15 TFLs at Louisiana Tech), plus secondary vets Zashon Rich, Donovan McIntosh, Koy Beasley, and Ja'son Prevard. Watch list: Johnson's decision-making, Gregory, the O-line.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Klein's QB-run-forward physicality, tailored to Johnson. Defense: speed-and-havoc front, question-mark depth. Strengths: QB ceiling, the schedule jackpot (misses Tech, BYU, AND Utah — projected favorite in eleven games, underdog only at TCU), no Dublin curse this year (Connelly's joke, but the 2025 Week 0 Ireland loss really did poison the season). Weaknesses: portal-built lines on both sides, first-year coordinators everywhere. Season goals: 9+ wins and the CCG — the path is genuinely open.

**3) Prediction.** 9-3 with a real shot at Arlington on tiebreakers and scheduling alone — the classic Big 12 "back into the title game, then shock someone on Friday night" candidate. If Johnson plays instinctively, this is the league's best dark horse. Klein's seat: a honeymoon with generational goodwill.

# TIER 3 — THE SWING CLASS

## Arizona Wildcats

**2025:** 9-4 (6-3) — the boom half of a program that only does booms and busts (top-30 or outside the top-75 in SP+ seven straight years). **SP+:** #30, 7.6 avg wins. **CBS:** 8-4 (losses at BYU, at Tech, Utah, at K-State).

**1) The roster & identity.** Noah Fifita enters Year 4 as the starter with a career line closing on legendary quiet: 9,183 yards, 73 TDs — Crawford's argument is that he's the most undersold player in the West, and his final campaign is the national coming-out. The offense around him retools: leading rusher and top two receivers gone, sophomore slot Gio Richardson and transfers Antwan Roberts (Marshall) and Cole Rusk (Illinois) step in, and four of seven line starters return with Washington and SJSU veteran adds. The defense is the spine — coordinator Danny Gonzales survived an injury-lottery 2025 (21 different starters) and returns eleven of them, led by edge Chase Kennedy, LB Taye Brown, and secondary playmakers Jay'Vion Cole and Gavin Hunter, reinforced by eleven transfers including USC's Matai Tagoa'i and Oregon's Daylen Austin. Field position is the hidden identity: the defense fed an inefficient offense great starting spots all year, and Brennan's fourth-down aggression cashed them.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Strengths: QB stability, defensive continuity, coaching rhythm in Year 3. Weaknesses: skill-corps proof, and the schedule — unlike the top three, Arizona plays EVERYBODY, opening the gauntlet at BYU on Sept. 12. Goals: survive the connectivity tax and play kingmaker; a 9-win repeat with this slate would be worth ten on anyone else's.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 — with the caveat that which contenders they beat (BYU? Utah at home? K-State?) will decide the CCG field. Brennan's seat is cool and warming in the good direction.

## TCU Horned Frogs

**2025:** 9-4 — two straight 9-4s, but tight-win dependent and 37th in SP+. **SP+:** #34, 7.1 avg wins. Sonny Dykes Year 5.

**1) The roster & identity.** The league's most fun experiment: Josh Hoover left for Indiana, and Dykes replaced him with HARVARD's Jaden Craig — 6-3, 230, 48 TDs against 10 picks over two Ivy seasons — running a new offense under UConn import Gordon Sammis, whose Huskies threw ONE interception all of last year. RB Jeremy Payne broke out late (476 yards over the final five), the receiver room mixes Jordan Dwyer, Ed Small, and zero-drop transfer Jeremy Scott, and LT Ben Taylor-Whitfield anchors a retooled line. The defense's 2025 split was comical — 19th against the run, 109th per dropback, no pass rush whatsoever — but six of seven linemen return, Paul Oyewale is the star-turn candidate, and transfers Cheta Ofili (from Tech!) and Koron Hayward bring elite pressure rates. Three transfer DBs rebuild the back.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals & the trap.** Strengths: coaching, offensive scheme upgrade, four soft openers (opponents all 58th-or-worse) for Craig's acclimation. Weaknesses: pass rush proof, secondary chemistry, and a NOVEMBER FROM HELL — four straight against the league's top five, including hosting BYU Oct. 3 and Utah Nov. 21. The trap: TCU opens against North Carolina in Dublin — and Connelly's beloved Dublin Loser Curse has wrecked the loser of that game every year it's existed. (For your ACC crossover segment: Belichick's UNC is on the other side. Someone's season starts cursed.)

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 — Craig is going to work, the November gauntlet is going to take two, and Dykes will quietly finish top-30 again while everyone argues about whether he's underrated. His seat: cool, with a "the title game was four years ago" hum underneath.

## Houston Cougars

**2025:** 10-3 — Willie Fritz's Year 2 rocket, right on his career pattern (.507 in Year 1s, .730 in Year 2s, .725 in Year 3s). **SP+:** #36, 7.4 avg wins.

**1) The roster & identity.** QB Conner Weigman returns off a 2,705-pass/795-rush/36-TD season, and Fritz reunited him with the Tulane band: RB Makhi Hughes (2,779 yards over two Tulane years before a lost Oregon detour) comes home to a Fritz-Nagle offense, Tulane guard Shadre Hurst and nickel Javion White follow, and the line adds all-conference transfers Drew Terrill and Anthony Boswell. Slot Amare Thomas and big-play sophomore Koby Young return; Oregon State's leading receiver Trent Walker joins. The insurance policy is outrageous: five-star freshman Keisean Henderson, a top-five national recruit, backs up Weigman. The defense returns its secondary core (Webb, Allen, James) and front-six vets (Mack, Fotu, Laufau) with an Oregon/Okie State/Yale tackle infusion.

**2) Strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Strengths: possibly the league's best combined trench play, QB continuity, a coach whose Year 3s are historically monsters. Weaknesses: the schedule (at Tech Week 3, at K-State, at Utah — all three contenders on the road), secondary depth post-McCutchin. Goals: crash the CCG conversation and make the Henderson succession a 2027 luxury, not a 2026 necessity.

**3) Prediction.** 8-4 with the Week 3 trip to Lubbock as the league's first great litmus test — cover the "Houston is the sleeper" take now, because if they steal that one, this becomes the race everyone wanted. Fritz at 66 is playing with house money and knows it.

## Oklahoma State Cowboys

**2025:** 1-11 (0-9) — the worst Cowboys team in 60-plus years, the end of the Gundy era. **2026 coach:** Eric Morris, first year — imported from North Texas with roughly half of the NATION'S NO. 1 OFFENSE. **SP+:** #39, 6.4 avg wins — Connelly calls it aggressive and admits he can't stop thinking they'll be really good anyway.

**1) The roster & identity.** The single most fascinating roster flip in the country: QB Drew Mestemaker (4,300 yards at UNT), RB Caleb Hawkins (1,400), WRs Wyatt Young (1,200) and Miles Coleman, and two all-conference linemen (Braydon Nelson, Johnny Dickson III) all followed Morris to Stillwater — plus explosive adds Tre Page III (7.5 ypc at Tarleton), Chris Barnes (Wake Forest — another ACC-doc crossover name), and Israel Polk. Connelly will be shocked if this isn't a top-20 offense. The defense decides everything: coordinator Skyler Cassity (the Sam Houston miracle-worker who made UNT competent) brings six Mean Green starters, UCLA LB Isaiah Chisom, and a stack of DII/backup fliers. Average defense = bowl and beyond; bad defense = fun 6-6.

**2) The frame.** Morris joins the Deion '23 / Cignetti '24 / Belichick '25 lineage of new-coach-mass-transfer projection nightmares — and Connelly's track record on those calls is good, which makes his open bullishness here notable. Seven conference games projected within a touchdown means the win total could swing wildly either way.

**3) Prediction.** 7-5 and the league's feel-good story — the offense travels, the defense is average enough, and improving on 1-11 by six games earns Morris coach-of-the-year votes he won't win. Watch the Oregon nonconference visit (from the Ducks' CBS win list) for a measuring-stick beatdown, then judge from October on.

# TIER 4 — THE PROVE-IT CLASS

## Arizona State Sun Devils
**2025:** 8-5 — an injury-shredded title defense salvaged by the Tech upset. **SP+:** #42, 6.0 wins. Dillingham's Year 4 is a full reset: 25 transfers in, only 11 of 34 regulars back, and a four-way QB derby (Kentucky's Cutter Boley the favorite over Mikey Keene, Cameron Dyer, and Jake Fette). The skill room rebuilds around 7.5-ypc backfield flashes (Kyson Brown, Jason Brown Jr.), Colorado's Omarion Miller, and Delaware State's Marquis Gillis; the line kept faith with returnees despite losing five of seven. Brian Ward's defense leans on C.J. Fite and transfers Emar'rion Winston and Ashton Stamps — with the fun wrinkle that ASU plays Kansas at WEMBLEY on Sept. 19 and visits Texas A&M (SEC-doc crossover) in nonconference. **Prediction:** 6-6 — the banked 2024 title buys Dillingham all the patience this rebuild needs, and Boley is a sneaky 2027 name.

## Cincinnati Bearcats 🔥-tepid
**2025:** 7-6 — the third straight year of modest gains. **SP+:** #50, 5.9 wins. Satterfield's whole 2026 is a bet on mid-major arbitrage: Georgia Southern QB JC French IV likely starts, the league's most-decorated returning O-line trio (Cotton, Tengesdahl, Tyo) protects him, and the skill corps and secondary are stitched from the MAC, FCS, and Division II. The elephant: the Sorsby gambling probe's Cincinnati chapter — the school is publicly denying it knew about his betting, and that story will not stay quiet. **The seat:** lukewarm; incremental improvement has bought time, but Connelly's ceiling question is the honest one, and 5-7 with this line would end the patience. **Prediction:** 6-6, bowl on a knife's edge.

## Baylor Bears 🔥🔥 (Hottest Seat in the Big 12)
**2025:** 5-7 despite a 3,681-yard QB season — the Bears lost EVERY game they didn't score 30, because the defense has averaged 92nd in SP+ for three straight years. **SP+:** #51, 5.9 wins (Vegas leans under at -108). **The full treatment:** Aranda is 36-37 in Year 7, 16-21 over three years, and Connelly flatly ranks the seat among the nation's hottest. The save-the-job plan is a spectacular double-bet: **DJ Lagway** at quarterback (the former No. 1 recruit, post-chaotic-portal-tour, with new OC Jake Spavital tasked with making the talent into a quarterback) and K-State's Joe Klanderman as the third DC hire (six K-State defenses averaging 37.7 in SP+, four Wildcats in tow including nickel Daniel Cobbs, plus Indiana champ DT Hosea Wheeler). The offense keeps almost nothing — three returnees with 200+ snaps — but the transfer starting XI has real talent. **How he escapes:** Lagway hits, the defense jumps 40 spots, 7-5 with a bowl — genuinely achievable, since eight of twelve games project within one score. **How it ends:** the close games break like they have for three years (Baylor's a favorite in only two of those eight coin flips), Lagway's turnover habit follows him, and Waco moves on in November. **Prediction:** 6-6 and a sweat — Lagway is the single highest-variance player in the conference, and this is the league's best weekly soap opera either way.

## Kansas Jayhawks 🔥-simmering
**2025:** 5-7, again — nine losses in eleven one-score games over two years, the cruelest stretch in the league. **SP+:** #55, 5.8 wins. Jalon Daniels is finally out of eligibility, and the offense hands itself to a derby (Cole Ballard vs. Isaiah Marshall vs. Rice's Chase Jenkins) with one massive tailwind: OC Andy Kotelnicki is BACK in Lawrence after his Penn State detour — KU had a top-30 offense in each of his last two years. ## Iowa State Cyclones

**2025:** 4-8 in Rich Rod's return — the transfer class Connelly loved mostly didn't click. **SP+:** #66, 5.2 wins. Rich Rod's seat: Year 2 grace, Year 3 questions loading.

## Colorado Buffaloes 🔥🔥 (The Complicated One)
**2025:** 3-9 (1-8) — the post-Shedeur/Hunter cliff, a rotating QB carousel that confused everything, and a season Deion Sanders spent managing serious health problems. **SP+:** #65, 4.6 wins — last in the league's projections and Vegas's win totals. **The full treatment:** this is Year 4, 16-21 overall, and a third-or-fourth total roster reset — an on-field case that would have any normal coach squarely on the hot seat. The counterweights are real too: the brand economics, the recruiting gravity, and a health situation that makes the whole conversation different in kind — handle it with care on air. The football case for optimism is Connelly's favorite contrarian take in the league: he likes almost everyone Deion added. New OC Brennan Marion (instant-improvement résumé), blue-chip redshirt freshman QB Julian Lewis, an outstanding transfer WR trio (Danny Scudero, Kam Perry, Texas's DeAndre Moore Jr.), seven veteran O-line adds, FBS tackling-machine LB Gideon Lampron (123 tackles, 18.5 TFLs), SEC-proven nickel Randon Fontenette, and a talent flier on Tennessee's exiled playmaker Boo Carter. Connelly's line: more proven production than even the nine-win 2024 roster had. **How this goes right:** Lewis-Marion clicks, 6-6, a bowl, and the program story flips overnight. **How it goes wrong:** the floor we saw last year, and the hardest conversation in the sport arrives. **Prediction:** 5-7, better than the record, with Lewis the reason to watch every week.
