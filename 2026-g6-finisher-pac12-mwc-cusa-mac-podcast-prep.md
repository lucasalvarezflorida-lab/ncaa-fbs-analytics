Sources synthesized: ESPN/Bill Connelly's Pac-12 (May 29), Mountain West (May 26), Conference USA (May 8), and MAC (May 1) previews + SP+ projections, Athlon's 2026 predictions and all-conference teams, CFN win totals, and current FanDuel/market numbers. ## The Shared Frame: The Bid Race, Full Hierarchy

Everything in these four leagues points at one prize — the CFP's fifth auto-bid for the highest-ranked G5/G6 champion — and the market has already drawn the map. FanDuel's to-make-the-CFP board: **Boise State +194** (miles clear of the field), Memphis/Tulane +820, **Liberty +1120**, James Madison +1300, Army and **UNLV +1600**, Navy +1800. Two of the top seven live in THIS doc — Boise in the reborn Pac-12 and UNLV in the gutted-but-game Mountain West — and Liberty's CUSA price is quietly third-shortest. The structural fact underneath: Connelly rates the new Pac-12 as the best-projected league in the G6 with the Mountain West second, so the bid probably runs through the West unless the American produces a runaway champion. Your season-long tracker: Boise's résumé versus everyone's.

One more shared theme worth naming on air: **the FCS invasion**. James Madison reached the CFP four years after moving up; now North Dakota State (Mountain West), Sacramento State (MAC), and the settled newcomers (Kennesaw, Jax State, Delaware, Missouri State in CUSA) are all running the same play. Connelly's line about NDSU — can the Bison do it in three? — is the whole era in one question.

# PART I: THE PAC-12 (IT'S BACK)

## Setting the Table

After two years as a two-team scheduling orphanage, the Pac-12 relaunches with eight teams: Oregon State and Washington State (110 years of membership between them) plus the winners of 13 of the last 14 Mountain West titles — Boise State, San Diego State, Fresno State, Utah State — along with Colorado State and Sun Belt import Texas State. It's the best projected league in the G6, with a scheduling quirk you'll want to explain on air: a seven-game round robin ends Nov. 21, followed by an adjustable **flex week** — everyone gets one repeat conference date, and the league can massage the matchups late to maximize a CFP résumé. (Boise's flex: at Utah State.) The anniversary hook writes your cold open: it's 20 years since the 2006 Fiesta Bowl — hook-and-ladder, Statue of Liberty, the game that proved mid-major miracles happen. The program that won it is now the entire G6's standard-bearer, again.

**2025 results (from their old homes):** SDSU 9-4 (SP+ 43, No. 5 special teams), Boise State 9-5 (won its seventh MW title in 15 years, beating UNLV 38-21 in the CG), Fresno State 9-4 (Entz Year 1), Texas State 7-6, Wazzu 7-6, Utah State 6-7, Oregon State 2-10 (fired Trent Bray mid-slide), Colorado State 2-10.

## The Featured Five

**Boise State (Danielson Year 4, 24-8).** The 2025 season was bipolar in a way you rarely see: +21.1 average margin in nine wins, -21.8 in five losses, with exactly ONE single-digit game all year. The 8-4 regular season (they opened AT Oregon-adjacent difficulty and got smoked in the bad weeks) still ended in a Mountain West title, which Connelly calls a pretty good disappointment. The 2026 case: Maddux Madsen returns for a fifth year and third as the starter, behind five of eight linemen and a Riley-Gaines backfield that combined for 2,157 scrimmage yards — the continuity is the best in the conference. The holes are identical on both sides: everyone out wide is new. The top four receivers are gone (senior slot Ben Ford plus sophomores and spring-star freshmen carry the room), and four of the top five DBs left — patched by the portal's best mid-major corner, Kennesaw State's JeRico Washington Jr. (13 breakups on a CUSA title team, and only a sophomore). The front six is legitimately nasty: Virgin-Morgan and Stege combined for 19 TFLs. CFN's projection: 10-2 with the opener at Oregon as the likely loss, plus real danger at Memphis-at-home and the trip to defending MAC champ Western Michigan — a nonconference slate built to bulletproof the bid. **Prediction: 10-2, Pac-12 champs, and the CFP bid — with the Oct. 10 trip to Fresno (where Boise has lost three straight to the Bulldogs) as the one league game that genuinely scares them.**

**San Diego State (Sean Lewis Year 3).** The 2025 formula was run-run-punt plus a top-five-in-everything defense — and now the awesome defense lost 11 of 17 regulars (four starters to power conferences, plus the coordinator AND the star punter) while the mediocre offense returns nearly whole. Denegal, Sutton, and Washington keep the identity; D-II tackling machine Cameron Cooper (81 tackles, 14 TFLs at Western Colorado) and a five-corner Big Sky haul restock the defense. **Prediction: 8-4 and second place — the special teams (No. 5 projected) steal a game, the pass rush costs them another.**

**Fresno State (Matt Entz Year 2).** Same bipolar profile as Boise (+19.6 in wins, -21.5 in losses), same identity as Entz's NDSU teams: run it, slow it, dare you to pass. Donelson and Luke return in the backfield, the defense leaped 77th→37th and returns its front (Jacks, Xepera, Laffitte plus a 13-TFL Sac State transfer), and the whole season is the QB derby — Maryland's Khristian Martin versus Jayden Mandal. Connelly's kicker is the honest one: the Bulldogs contend if they have a quarterback, and he's not sure they do. **Prediction: 8-4 with the season-defining fortnight built right in — Boise visits Oct. 10, then Fresno's at SDSU Oct. 17. Win both and the Valley hosts the title race.**

**Washington State (Kirby Moore Year 1).** The transience is the story — fourth head coach in six seasons, yet five straight years ranked 47th-68th. The roster bet is Connelly's favorite kind: UC Davis sophomore QB Caden Pinnick (3,206 pass / 437 rush / 35 TDs as a FRESHMAN — one of his five favorite transfers in the league), five transfer pass-catchers, and a defense replacing ALL 17 players with 200+ snaps under new coordinator... Trent Bray, the man Oregon State just fired as head coach. The Bray-coaches-against-OSU-twice storyline (regular season AND possibly the flex week) is the pettiest subplot in the league. **Prediction: 6-6 — Pinnick is real, the defense is a full year away.**

**Texas State (GJ Kinne Year 4).** The almost-team: four gut-wrenching losses in five weeks (two TDs allowed in the final 80 seconds vs. Arkansas State, a blown 28-3 lead vs. Troy, a double-OT Marshall loss) turned a division-title-caliber season into 7-6 — and, in Connelly's sharp observation, probably kept Kinne AND QB Brad Jackson in San Marcos, because the P4 stopped calling. Jackson posted the fourth-best Total QBR in the G6 (3,224 pass / 890 rush) — the best of any returnee — with both 1,000-yard-adjacent targets (Beau Sparks, Chris Dawn Jr.) back. The No. 26 projected offense meets the No. 129 projected defense; McNeese DT Phillip Bradford (15.5 TFLs at 308 pounds) is the one genuine fix. **Prediction: 7-5 and the league's must-watch chaos — they'll win a shootout against a contender and lose one 45-42 to somebody they shouldn't.**

## Quick Hits: The Rest

- **Oregon State (Shephard Year 1):** The extremes job — 13 seasons of nine-plus wins OR losses in the last 31 years. Maalik Murphy (yes, the Texas/Duke arm) gives the offense a floor, the run front (360-pound Jacob Schuster!) is stout, and EIGHT projected one-score games mean 8-4 and 4-8 are both live. Prediction: 6-6, because chaos deserves respect.
- **Utah State (Mendenhall Year 2):** By Bronco standards, 89th in returning production is luxury. Robert Anae reunion at OC, McCae Hillstead at QB, 19 defensive transfers — but the schedule front-loads Washington, Utah, and Boise. Prediction: 5-7 with late-season competence.
- **Colorado State (Mora Year 1):** The UConn caravan (Farrar, two starters, Diomande) meets TCU-transfer QB Hauss Hejny in a genuinely open derby. One winning season in eight years is the bar to clear. Prediction: 5-7, better by November.

# PART II: THE MOUNTAIN WEST (LIFE AFTER BOISE)

## Setting the Table

The Mountain West lost its entire aristocracy in one realignment swing — Boise State, SDSU, Fresno State, Utah State, and Colorado State all left for the Pac-12 — and answered by holding onto rising UNLV, adding UTEP (CUSA) and Northern Illinois (MAC), and landing the biggest FCS name in the sport: NORTH DAKOTA STATE. The new lineup is 10 teams: Air Force, Hawai'i, Nevada, New Mexico, Northern Illinois, North Dakota State, San José State, UNLV, UTEP, Wyoming. Connelly still rates it the G6's second-best league, and the vacuum at the top makes it the most genuinely open race in the sport's second tier.

The wrinkle you MUST explain on air: **NDSU is not eligible for the Mountain West championship during its FBS transition** — the Bison could go undefeated and be locked out of both the title game and (absent an NCAA waiver) the playoff. So the league has two races running simultaneously: the official one, and the "is the ineligible team actually the best team" shadow race. Connelly's framing sets the stakes: JMU needed four years from FCS to the CFP — can the Bison do it in three?

**2025 context:** Boise beat UNLV 38-21 in the final Boise-era title game — UNLV's THIRD straight championship-game loss to the Broncos. New Mexico, the league's lowest-projected team a year ago, went 9-4 (6-2) under first-year coach Jason Eck with its first bowl since 2016. UNLV went 10-4 in Dan Mullen's debut, running its three-year total to 30 wins at a program that had 18 losing seasons in 19 years before 2023.

## The Featured Three (Plus the Shadow Team)

**UNLV (Mullen Year 2, 10-4).** The formula, per CFN, is fully operational: power-conference coaching, a dynamic offense, just enough defense — 30 wins in three years, three straight title games, and the Boise problem solved by realignment. Jackson Arnold's senior season is the league's biggest QB story, Jai'Den Thomas anchors the backfield, and the Rebels are a legitimately respectable 5-3 against P4 teams over three years, with a Cal game this fall that doubles as the bid-résumé linchpin. The schedule is spicy: Memphis in Week 0 (an inter-doc crossover with your American prep), at Hawai'i Week 1, NDSU at home Oct. 10, at Air Force Oct. 17 — Connelly's best-games list is basically UNLV's road slate. The "how is UNLV not in the Big 12 or Pac-12" discourse is a real segment; the on-field product keeps making the question louder. **Prediction: 9-3 (CFN agrees), the Mountain West title at last — and a season-long staring contest with Boise for the bid, decided by whose losses look worse in November.**

**New Mexico (Jason Eck Year 2).** The best story in the league: lowest-projected team to 9-4 (6-2) and the program's first bowl since 2016, and now the FUTURES FAVORITE at +220 — ahead of UNLV — on the strength of one of the league's best returning foundations. Betting markets moving a program from worst-projected to favorite in twelve months basically never happens; the Lobos earned it. **Prediction: 9-3 and the title game — the Oct. 24 NDSU visit and the UNLV date decide whether Albuquerque hosts the whole thing.**

**Wyoming (Jay Sawvel Year 3) — the league's real hot seat.** 7-17 through two seasons, and the program that defined Mountain West physicality under Bohl needs a breakthrough. The response: former Bills assistant Christian Taylor at OC, William & Mary transfer QB Tyler Hughes, and a genuinely favorable schedule. The stakes are explicit — 7-5 and a bowl is survival; another losing year and Laramie moves on. **Prediction: 6-6, agonizing, seat unresolved into December.**

**North Dakota State (Polasek) — the shadow team.** The Bison arrive with a championship culture and a road gauntlet — at Air Force, at UNLV, at New Mexico, at Hawai'i, all four fellow contenders away from Fargo, per Athlon. Every NDSU result is a referendum: on the transition rules, on the FCS-to-CFP pipeline, and on whether the sport's most consistent program travels. **Prediction: 8-4-ish against the toughest possible welcome, with at least one contender scalp — and a rules-debate segment every time they win.**

## Quick Hits: The Rest

- **Hawai'i:** +400 says the market believes; the defense taking one more step is the entire thesis, and the Week 1 UNLV visit to the islands is the league's opening statement game. Prediction: 8-4.
- **Air Force:** Szarka plus the academy regression-to-excellence pattern; last year's 51-48 loss to UNLV was one of the season's most delirious games, and Connelly formally requested a repeat (Oct. 17). Prediction: 7-5.
- **San José State (Niumatalolo) and Nevada (Choate):** parallel builds, bowl-bubble ceilings. Predictions: 6-6 and 5-7.
- **Northern Illinois & UTEP:** the imports. NIU's physicality should translate better than UTEP's three-straight-nine-loss baseline. Predictions: 5-7 and 4-8.

# PART III: CONFERENCE USA (THE COIN-FLIP LEAGUE)

## Setting the Table

Connelly's opening stat is the league's whole identity: only two conferences saw HALF their league games finish within one score last season — the SEC and Conference USA — and it's CUSA's third straight year at 45% or higher. Add Tuesday/Wednesday night October games and you have the sport's best background-noise product, now trimmed to 10 teams (Louisiana Tech to the Sun Belt, UTEP to the Mountain West): Delaware, FIU, Jacksonville State, Kennesaw State, Liberty, Middle Tennessee, Missouri State, New Mexico State, Sam Houston, Western Kentucky.

**2025:** Kennesaw State completed a 10-losses-to-10-wins surge and won the championship over reigning champ Jacksonville State — behind QB Amari Odom (the league's efficiency king down the stretch) and leading receiver Gabriel Benyard — while Jax State's Cam Cook led the NATION in rushing before leaving for West Virginia (your Big 12 doc crossover). Sam Houston did the exact inverse of Kennesaw (10 wins to 10 losses); preseason favorite Liberty face-planted to 4-8 in a season Connelly calls downright strange; WKU banked its fifth straight year of eight or nine wins; and FBS newcomers Delaware and Missouri State went a combined 14-12, immediately competent.

## The Featured Storylines

**The title race is a genuine six-pack.** Connelly gives Western Kentucky, Liberty, Kennesaw, Jax State, Delaware, and FIU all real paths, with four teams within 0.7 projected wins of the top. Athlon's call: WKU over Liberty in the championship game. The head-to-head skeleton: Oct. 7 Jax State at Kennesaw (the CG rematch), Oct. 21 Liberty at Kennesaw on a WEDNESDAY (the Flames' third road tossup in four games — the rebound gets stress-tested immediately), Nov. 14 Jax State at WKU.

**Liberty is the value play and the bid sleeper.** At +1120 for the CFP — third-shortest in the G6 — the market prices the Flames' 2025 as the aberration, not the level. A rebound-conducive schedule plus that defensive line (Nathan Pirolli, Mike Jarvis) makes 10-2 live, and a 12-1 Liberty is the only CUSA résumé that could crash the Boise-versus-American bid fight.

**Kennesaw State is the proof-of-concept.** From 10 losses to a title in twelve months, a September trip to eventual national champion Indiana on the 2025 résumé, and now the target on their back — with Boise having poached their best corner (JeRico Washington Jr.), a perfect food-chain story connecting this doc's two halves.

**The Miami thread lives at FIU.** Your listeners' crosstown neighbor is the league's rising team: sixth in Athlon's projections with the most all-conference selections of anyone — WRs Jojo Stone and Greg Gaines, TE Jackson Verdugo, OL Zaire Flournoy, DLs Dante Anderson and Kenton Simmons, CB Jai-Ayviauynn Celestine, safeties Demetrius Hill and Shamir Sterlin — plus a home date with Buffalo as a nonconference oddity. If the Panthers crash the CG race, that's a weekly local segment 20 minutes from your listeners' couches.

# PART IV: THE MAC (LIFE IS HARD, CELEBRATE IT)

## Setting the Table

Connelly opens his MAC preview like a motivational speaker at a Rust Belt truck stop: MAC life is hard, the November midweek weather is hard, the money is hard — and that's the point. The 2026 twist he loves most: **Sacramento State** — from actual Sacramento, California — looked at this hard Midwestern life and chose it anyway, jumping up from FCS to replace Northern Illinois (off to the Mountain West). The league's nonconference gauntlet is its identity: this season alone, Kent State and Ball State play Ohio State, Central Michigan plays BOTH Miamis (Ohio and Florida — yes, the Chippewas visit Hard Rock, more below), Western Michigan plays Michigan, and Buffalo visits Penn State. Somebody in this league will beat a power team; somebody will then survive frozen Tuesday nights in November to lift the trophy at Ford Field.

**2025:** Western Michigan won the MAC championship — Lance Taylor's best Broncos team since the P.J. Fleck 2016 run — over a Miami (Ohio) program that has now reached Detroit three straight years. Four teams finished with two or fewer league losses. Toledo lost Jason Candle to UConn after a decade; Kent State overachieved at 5-7; the league stayed the league.

## The Featured Storylines

**Western Michigan is the G6 sleeper conversation.** CFN's preview literally asks whether this is the best Group of Six team. The champs return the MAC's best secondary (corner Javarius Smith, safety Joshua Franklin), rode 24 takeaways to a 9-1 record when forcing at least one, and get the season's most delicious nonconference stakes: **Boise State comes to Kalamazoo** — the +194 bid favorite visiting the defending MAC champion is the single biggest G6 crossover game of the year, a genuine bid-race elimination match in September. The rebuild job: the entire pass rush is gone (MAC-best 14.5-sack Tucker among all seven multi-sack Broncos), replaced by portal volume. **Prediction: 9-3, back to Ford Field — and if they shock Boise, the whole bid hierarchy detonates.**

**Toledo is a full-scale Mercer transplant.** New coach Mike Jacobs brought his FCS operation north wholesale, headlined by DE Andrew Zock — SoCon Freshman of the Year, then the Buck Buchanan Award (FCS' best defender) with 11.5 sacks — plus Mercer's linebackers (Prince, Fox), nickel (Watkins), RB (CJ Miller), and WR (first-team All-SoCon Adjatay Dabbs). The one holdover who matters most: QB John Alan Richter, who waited three years behind Finn and Gleason and finally gets the keys, now with Michigan transfer Anthony Simpson out wide. The defense replaces literally zero returning starters from a unit that allowed 3.82 yards a play in league games. One preview's perfect line: it's Toledo in name only — but of all the 2026 transplant experiments, this one has the best chance of working immediately. **Prediction: 8-4 with the Oct. 24 home date against Western Michigan as the prove-it game.**

**Miami (Ohio) keeps showing up.** Three straight trips to Detroit, the league's most proven offensive line, and Connelly's favorite MAC transfer: Monmouth RB Rodney Nelson (1,802 yards, three 200-yard games) — he'll be shocked if Nelson isn't a star. The Nov. 24 trip to Western Michigan, one week before the title game, is a potential straight-up elimination bout. **Prediction: 8-4 and Detroit, again.**

**Central Michigan plays at Miami. Your Miami.** Matt Drinkall's Year 2 Chippewas — 7-6 in a debut where he maximized everything — play both Miamis, including the trip to Hard Rock Stadium. That's a direct Canes-content bridge: a MAC opponent your listeners will actually scout. (CMU also plays at New Mexico and hosts Wyoming — this doc eats its own tail.) **Prediction: 7-5, playing spoiler in the Cluster.**

# THE FULL-MAP CLOSER: YOUR TEN-CONFERENCE LIBRARY
