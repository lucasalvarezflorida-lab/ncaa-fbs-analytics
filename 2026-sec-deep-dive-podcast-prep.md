# 2026 SEC Football Deep Dive — Podcast Prep

*Compiled July 17, 2026 — three days before SEC Media Days opens in Tampa (July 20–23, Tampa Marriott Water Street / JW Marriott — first time the event has ever been held in Florida). Sources synthesized: ESPN/Bill Connelly's SEC preview + SP+ projections (June 24), CBS Sports game-by-game projections (Brad Crawford) and win-total picks (John Talty), USA TODAY's SEC hot seat rankings, 247Sports portal class rankings, and current FanDuel/BetMGM numbers.*

## Setting the Table: What Happened in 2025 (60-Second Recap)

The SEC's championship drought hit three years — the Big Ten has now won three straight national titles (Michigan, Ohio State, and 16-0 Indiana) — and the league spent all offseason hearing about it. The 2025 SEC race was a pileup: seven teams finished 6-2 or 7-1 in the final year of the eight-game schedule, five of them made the CFP (Georgia, Alabama, Ole Miss, Texas A&M, Oklahoma), and only one — Ole Miss — reached a semifinal. Nobody made the title game.

Georgia won the conference again, losing at Alabama in the regular season and then flattening the Tide 28-7 in the Atlanta rematch — Kirby's third SEC title in four years. Texas A&M ran unbeaten into Thanksgiving weekend before Texas ended the perfect season, then lost its CFP first-round game. Oklahoma rode an elite Venables defense (and two season-saving defensive touchdowns — a 71-yard fumble return to beat Tennessee by six, an 87-yard pick-six to beat Alabama by two) back to the playoff. Texas, the preseason No. 1, lost to 4-8 Florida, missed the field entirely, and then thumped Michigan in the Citrus Bowl once Arch Manning caught fire — he had the nation's best QBR from November on. Vanderbilt won ten games and came within about three points of a playoff berth in Diego Pavia's finale.

And then there's the story that swallowed the sport: **Lane Kiffin left Ole Miss for LSU on the eve of the playoff.** Baton Rouge fired Brian Kelly in October, came for Kiffin after the Egg Bowl, and got him — before his 11-1 Rebels played a single CFP game. Defensive coordinator Pete Golding took over, beat Tulane in the first round, beat Georgia in the Sugar Bowl quarterfinal in New Orleans, and came within one play of the national championship game before falling to Miami in the Fiesta Bowl semifinal — all while Kiffin was, not so subtly, recruiting his own former players to Baton Rouge mid-run. The fallout is still radioactive in July.

**The 2025 ledger:** Georgia 12-2 (8-1), Ole Miss 13-2 (7-1), Alabama 11-4 (7-2), Texas A&M 11-2 (7-1), Vanderbilt 10-3 (6-2), Oklahoma 10-3 (6-2), Texas 10-3 (6-2), Tennessee 8-5, Missouri 8-5, LSU 7-6, Auburn 5-7, Kentucky 5-7, Mississippi State 5-8, Florida 4-8, South Carolina 4-8, Arkansas 2-10.

**What's new for 2026:**

- **The nine-game SEC schedule arrives.** Every team plays nine league games for the first time. Connelly's projections don't have a single SEC team above 9.8 average wins because of it, and the whole conference is bracing for a committee argument about a pile of 9-3 résumés.
- **Six new head coaches** — the biggest single-cycle turnover in SEC history: Lane Kiffin (LSU), Jon Sumrall (Florida), Alex Golesh (Auburn), Ryan Silverfield (Arkansas), Will Stein (Kentucky), and Pete Golding elevated at Ole Miss. Fired or departed: Kelly, Napier, Freeze, Pittman, Stoops... and Kiffin, who fired himself out of Oxford.
- **The injunction era.** Trinidad Chambliss sued the NCAA in Mississippi court and won an extra year after being denied; Oklahoma LB Owen Heinecke won one too; Tennessee's Joey Aguilar was denied. The NCAA has since passed an age-based 5-for-5 eligibility reform. Roster rules are being written by judges in real time.
- **The Cocktail Party moves to Atlanta** while Jacksonville's stadium is renovated — Georgia-Florida in Mercedes-Benz this year, Tampa in the rotation next.

## The Playoff Picture

- **CBS (Crawford) projects four SEC bids:** Texas (No. 2 seed, SEC champ), Georgia (No. 5), LSU (No. 9), Ole Miss (No. 11) — with 9-3 Alabama as the first team out, left arguing schedule strength to the committee.
- **SP+ (Connelly):** Georgia 9.8 projected wins and a 31.9% chance at 11+; no one else is above 8.1%. Ten SEC teams project top-20 nationally, none in the top three — the exact same shape as 2025, which produced five bids. Connelly's contender list: Georgia, Texas, Texas A&M, LSU, Alabama.
- **Vegas:** Texas is the only SEC team in the national title top tier (+750, tied Indiana). The league's championship drought is officially priced in — books have Ohio State, Notre Dame, and the Buckeyes/Hoosiers/Ducks Big Ten trio ahead of or level with everyone in the South.
- **The structural story:** the nine-game schedule is a bid-count wildcard. Five bids at eight games; does nine games mean six teams with strong résumés, or a circular firing squad that leaves the SEC with three? Connelly's take, paraphrased: the brutal schedules are what the SEC's money pays for, and they don't entitle anyone to a bid after too many losses.

## Sidebar: The Kiffin Cold War (Your Best Recurring Segment)

This feud touches four programs and will not die. The timeline, for your listeners:

1. **October 2025:** LSU fires Brian Kelly mid-season.
2. **Nov. 30, 2025:** Kiffin leaves 11-1, playoff-bound Ole Miss for LSU — before the CFP. Ole Miss promotes Pete Golding.
3. **December–January:** While Golding wins two playoff games, Kiffin openly courts Ole Miss players. He lands star edge Princewill Umanmielen (the portal's No. 1 edge rusher), LB TJ Dottery, and WR Winston Watkins in Baton Rouge. But Golding retains the four biggest names: Trinidad Chambliss, Kewan Lacy, Will Echoles, and Suntarine Perkins.
4. **Spring 2026:** Dabo Swinney accuses Ole Miss of tampering; the NCAA opens a probe — so Golding's white-hat status has an asterisk of its own.
5. **July 2026:** Chambliss publicly calls Kiffin's account of Ole Miss's recruiting resources not truthful. This is still going at Media Days.
6. **Sept. 19 (Week 3): LSU at Ole Miss.** Possibly the most hostile environment of the college football season. Circle it in red. Then Kiffin gets Texas A&M at home the very next week (Sept. 26).

Bonus wrinkle for a Canes-centric show: Ole Miss's season ended against Miami in the Fiesta semifinal, and Miami's Carson Beck era ended against Indiana a week later. You have a natural "we were the final boss of the Kiffin saga" angle.

# TIER 1 — THE FAVORITES

## Georgia Bulldogs

**2025:** 12-2 (8-1), SEC champions, lost the Sugar Bowl CFP quarterfinal to Ole Miss. **SP+:** #4, 9.8 avg wins. **CBS:** 11-1 regular season (lone loss: at South Carolina — a genuinely spicy upset pick), loses SEC CG to Texas, No. 5 seed. **Vegas:** 9.5 total, +310.

**1) The roster.** This is the continuity monster: 68% returning production (top-10 nationally), a league-high 169 returning starts, and 13 of the 18 defenders who logged 200+ snaps back. QB Gunner Stockton returns to run what Connelly memorably calls a "blue-chip service academy offense" — extreme efficiency, sideline-to-sideline stress, minimal risk. Backs Nate Frazier and Chauncey Bowens return, with Kentucky bruiser Dante Dowdell added via portal, behind seven linemen with starting experience (four of them sophomores). The portal adds were surgical rather than splashy: Georgia Tech deep threat Isiah Canion (14.6 ypc) to attack the big-play problem, Clemson safety Khalil Barnes for the back end. The defense returns a terrifying sophomore trio — DTs Elijah Griffin and Joseph Jonah-Ajonye plus CB Ellis Robinson IV — and Connelly calls the tackle rotation the deepest in the country. Watch list: Griffin (potential No. 1-pick trajectory), Robinson, London Humphreys (15.3 ypc as the returning field-stretcher).

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Mike Bobo conservatism — quick game, huge success rate, allergic to explosives. That's the entire Georgia debate in one sentence: they've finished sixth in SP+ two straight years, they've lost two straight playoff games, and the culprit both times is an offense that doesn't generate big plays even though the defense doesn't allow any. Defense: no unit in the conference gets more benefit of the doubt — eight games allowing 14 or fewer points last year with a young lineup that's now experienced. Strengths: DT depth, offensive efficiency, special teams (3rd in SP+), the schedule (avoids Texas, A&M, and LSU in the regular season and is projected favorite in all 12 games). Weakness: explosiveness, full stop. Season goals: a fifth straight trip to Atlanta is close to assumed; the real goal is winning three playoff games in December/January, which requires an offense that can score fast when trailing.

**3) Prediction.** The floor is the highest in America: 10-2 is the realistic worst case. I'd take the CBS shape — 11-1, SEC title game — but I'd flip the CG result and take Georgia over Texas, because Kirby is 3-0 against Sark's Longhorns including the 2024 title game. Call it 11-1, SEC champs, No. 2/3 seed, and a semifinal-or-better run. The Nov. 7 trip to Oxford is the revenge game of the SEC season (Golding is 1-0 lifetime against Kirby, from the Sugar Bowl). Hot seat: nonexistent — but note the streak on the line: Kirby hasn't won fewer than 10 in a full season since 2016.

## Texas Longhorns

**2025:** 10-3 (6-2), preseason No. 1, missed the CFP, throttled Michigan in the Citrus Bowl. **SP+:** #6, 8.6 avg wins — with the No. 1 schedule in America. **CBS:** 12-1, SEC champs, No. 2 seed (losses: Ohio State opener only... then beats Georgia in Atlanta). **Vegas:** 9.5 total (Talty likes the over at +110), +300 — the SEC favorite. Arch Manning is the Heisman favorite.

**1) The roster.** Arch is back for what's likely his final season, and Sarkisian built the most talked-about portal class in the sport around him — 15 players, almost all of them ranked at the top of their position: Cam Coleman (No. 1 portal WR, from Auburn), Hollywood Smothers (No. 2 RB, from NC State — 4.4 yards after contact per carry, a comical number), Raleek Brown (No. 5 RB, Arizona State), Melvin Siani (No. 3 OT), Rasheem Biles (No. 3 LB, from Pitt — Connelly's favorite transfer in the entire class: 15.8% tackle rate, 15 TFLs, 4.5 sacks, 2 INTs), Bo Mascoe (No. 7 CB, Rutgers), Ian Geffrard (No. 9 DL). Returning: WRs Ryan Wingo and Emmett Mosley V, star edge Colin Simmons (12 sacks), S Jelani McDonald, nickel Graceson Littleton, and 130 returning starts (68% returning production, 6th nationally). Key losses: LBs Anthony Hill Jr. and Liona Lefau, DC Pete Kwiatkowski — replaced, eyebrow-raisingly, by Will Muschamp, whose last top-10 defense was 2014. Watch list: Coleman (highest ceiling in the portal, 7.9% drop rate is the flaw), Simmons, sophomore CB Kade Phillips, freshman WR room (Kaliq Lockett, Jermaine Bishop Jr.).

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Sark's pro-style spread finally has the pieces again after sliding from 6th (2023) to 30th in offensive SP+ — Connelly says there are no excuses left for anything outside the top 10. Arch's lone remaining flaw is accuracy throwing on the run; everything else clicked in November. Defense: ranked ahead of the offense two straight years, which makes firing Kwiatkowski for Muschamp the single riskiest coordinator hire in the league. Strengths: skill-position matchup advantages on every snap, edge rush, secondary depth, the Arch factor. Weaknesses: overall depth (Sark churned so much roster that a couple of injuries bite hard), the Muschamp gamble, and a schedule from hell — eight projected top-20 opponents, with the last three (at LSU Nov. 14, at A&M Nov. 27) on the road. Season goals: title or bust, explicitly. CBS calls it exactly that, on one of the nation's most expensive rosters.

**3) Prediction.** The talent says 12-1; the schedule says 9-3 wouldn't shock anyone — that spread is the widest in the conference. My read: 10-2, in Atlanta, in the CFP either way — and the Ohio State opener is nearly a free roll (a loss there costs almost nothing in January given the rest of the résumé). Sark isn't on any hot seat, but the subtext is real: Texas has NEVER won the SEC, Arch likely leaves after this year, and a second straight miss with this roster would change the temperature in Austin overnight. Heisman note for your picks segment: Arch at short odds is bad value; Coleman or Stockton late-round flyers are the play.

# TIER 2 — LOADED AND DANGEROUS

## Texas A&M Aggies

**2025:** 11-2 (7-1) — unbeaten until Texas ruined Thanksgiving, then a CFP first-round exit. **SP+:** #9, 8.4 avg wins. **CBS:** 8-4 (losses at LSU, at Oklahoma, Texas... and Alabama flips to a road win). **Vegas:** 8.5 (Talty: over), +850.

**1) The roster.** The skill talent that carried an 11-win team is nearly all back: QB Marcel Reed, RB Rueben Owens II, WRs Mario Craver and Ashton Bethel-Roman, plus defensive anchors T.J. Searcy, Dezz Ricks, Marcus Ratcliffe, and Dalton Brooks. LB Daymion Sanford returns but is expected to miss time with injury. What's gone is enormous in a different way: 13 NFL Combine invites, OC Collin Klein, and — this is the story — six of the top seven offensive linemen and five of seven on the defensive line. Elko rebuilt both trenches almost entirely through the portal: four projected transfer OL starters (42 combined SEC starts last year, so at least they're battle-tested), plus five D-line transfers headlined by SDSU's Ryan Henderson and Northwestern's Anto Saka (12.5% pressure rate). Tulsa tackling machine Ray Coney arrives at LB. Two new coordinators: Holmon Wiggins (offense), Lyle Hemphill (defense). Watch list: Craver, Owens, Saka, and freshman-laden depth (21 redshirt freshmen — a modern roster-building flex).

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Reed's dual-threat rhythm game — he was one of only three SEC QBs with 3,000 passing and 550 rushing yards (Chambliss and Pavia the others). The problem: seventh in the league in QBR because of pure mistakes — 12 INTs, four fumbles, a 15th-of-16 catchable-ball rate, and a nightmarish stat line in the two losses (9.3 yards per completion, 0 TD, 4 INT). Defense: Elko's scheme with elite back-seven talent and a total mystery up front. Strengths: returning skill talent, secondary, Elko/GM Derek Miller's roster-building track record, elite 2027 recruiting momentum (A&M currently holds the No. 1 class). Weaknesses: portal-rebuilt lines (Connelly's warning: rebuilding a line through the portal is how otherwise-good teams die), Reed's turnover glitches, six projected top-20 opponents with four on the road. Season goals: prove 2025 wasn't schedule-flavored — CBS notes how weak last year's slate was — and win the games that stamp a contender: LSU, OU, Texas.

**3) Prediction.** This is the widest gap on the board: SP+ top-10 team, CBS 8-4 team. Split it: 9-3 with the season defined by Sept. 26 in Baton Rouge and Black Friday vs. Texas. If the O-line transfers gel by October, the CFP is live; if Reed throws multi-pick games again, 8-4 and a January of what-ifs. No heat whatsoever on Elko — he's got the juice, the GM structure, and the recruiting board.

## LSU Tigers

**2025:** 7-6 (3-5) — Kelly fired in October. **2026 coach:** Lane Kiffin, Year 1. **SP+:** #10, 8.5 avg wins. **CBS:** 10-2, No. 9 CFP seed — with the delicious detail that the two losses come at Ole Miss and at Tennessee, his two former SEC schools. **Vegas:** 8.5 (Talty: over, -150), +850.

**1) The roster.** The No. 1 transfer portal class in America, full stop — the self-styled Portal King's magnum opus after four straight top-five classes at Ole Miss. The headliners: QB Sam Leavitt (Arizona State — the portal's No. 1 QB, the 2024 ASU playoff-run engine before injuries wrecked his 2025; Kiffin literally flew to meet him to close the deal), plus heir apparent Husan Longstreet (USC five-star). Four explosive transfer WRs: Jayce Brown (Kansas State, a monstrous 3.0 yards per route run), Jackson Harris (Hawai'i), Tre' Brown III (ODU), Winston Watkins (Ole Miss) — plus Eugene Wilson III poached from Florida. OT Jordan Seaton (Colorado — zero sacks allowed, 1.1% blown-block rate) anchors a rebuilt line. On defense: Ole Miss edge Princewill Umanmielen (portal's No. 1 edge), Tennessee edge Jordan Ross, Auburn DT Malik Blocton, Ole Miss LB TJ Dottery, Boise State S Ty Benefield (105 tackles) join returnees Whit Weeks, Dominick McKinley, DJ Pickett, and Harold Perkins' departed shadow. Blue-chip freshmen Lamar Brown, Richard Anderson, and Havon Finney Jr. give it depth. Returning production: just 61%, 89 returning starts — this is a build-a-bear roster. Watch list: Leavitt, Umanmielen, TE Trey'Dez Green (the unlock candidate), Weeks.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Kiffin/Charlie Weis Jr. tempo-and-leverage passing — Kiffin coaxes top-25 offense out of nearly any QB, and Leavitt is far better than "any QB." Defense: Blake Baker's aggressive pressure system — two top-15 SP+ defenses in the past three years between Mizzou and LSU, and SP+ projects this unit No. 2 nationally. Strengths: pass-catcher explosiveness, defensive front talent, coordinator pedigree on both sides. Weaknesses: O-line depth is genuinely precarious (only two of last year's top seven back), unproven RBs (Durham and Berry haven't matched the recruiting hype), and total roster cohesion risk — new coach, new QB, 136 incoming starts. Season goals: LSU hasn't finished top-10 in SP+ since the 2019 title season. Kiffin was not hired to compete for playoff spots; he was hired for championships, and CBS says no first-year coach in America faces more pressure.

**3) Prediction.** The schedule front-loads the drama: Clemson in Week 1, at Ole Miss Week 3, A&M Week 4, and the closing stretch brings Alabama and Texas to Baton Rouge. I'll say 9-3 and a bubble sweat rather than CBS's clean 10-2 — transfer-heavy teams usually leak one game they shouldn't. But the ceiling is a title, and the floor (7-5 with a locker-room chemistry story) would be the loudest story in the sport. Hot seat: obviously not in Year 1 — but Kiffin has imported championship-now expectations, a hated-rival backstory, and a fan base that fired Kelly at 7-5ish pace. There is no honeymoon clause in that contract.

## Alabama Crimson Tide

**2025:** 11-4 (7-2) — beat Georgia in Tuscaloosa, lost the SEC CG rematch 28-7, won a CFP first-round game, lost the quarterfinal. Also lost the opener to Florida State and collapsed late. **SP+:** #11, 8.0 avg wins. **CBS:** 9-3, first team out of the CFP (losses: Georgia, A&M, at LSU). **Vegas:** 8.5 (Talty: over), +850.

**1) The roster.** This is the scariest continuity profile of any blue blood: 48% returning production (90th nationally), 35% on offense — dead last in the conference tier. Gone: QB Ty Simpson (NFL), leading rusher Jam Miller, three of Simpson's top four targets, and six of the top seven offensive linemen. DeBoer deliberately skipped the veteran-QB portal market and is handing the offense to either redshirt freshman **Keelon Russell** — the No. 2 overall prospect in the 2025 class, nearly flawless in a tiny 2025 sample — or longtime DeBoer backup Austin Mack. The only notable skill transfer, NC State WR Noah Rogers, is already hurt (spring injury, out to start the season). The defense is the inverse: Kane Wommack returns maybe the best secondary in America — CBs Zabien Brown and Dijon Lee Jr., nickel Red Morgan, safeties Bray Hubbard and Keon Sabb — plus potential All-American edge Yhonzae Pierre. But four of the top five interior D-linemen are gone, patched with transfers Devan Thompkins (USC), Kedrick Bingley-Jones (Miss State), and Terrance Green (Oregon). Watch list: Russell (the whole season), Pierre, sophomore skill wave (Lotzeir Brooks, Rico Scott, Derek Meadows, Kaleb Edwards), freshman RB Ezavier Crowell.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: DeBoer's rhythm-and-spacing passing game — except last year it finished 36th in offensive SP+, the run game was dreadful, and the No. 1 receiver had the country's worst high-volume drop rate (13.0%). Now it's the same scheme with a teenager at the wheel and a rebuilt line. Defense: top-five SP+ projection, elite coverage, aggressive structure. Strengths: secondary, edge rush, the sheer recruiting talent stockpile, a soft launch (no projected top-20 opponent until Week 6 — which is Georgia, Oct. 10). Weaknesses: total offensive inexperience, interior lines both ways, special teams (107th last year, projected 109th — a real hidden-points leak), and early landmines: Kentucky Week 2, an FSU revenge game Week 3 (they lost that opener last year), South Carolina Week 4. Season goals: DeBoer has lost as many games in two years as Saban lost in his final five. Year 3 is the referendum.

**3) Prediction & the seat.** The honest range: if Russell is the generational player his recruiting ranking says, this is a 10-2 playoff team and the QB story of the year. If he's a normal redshirt freshman behind a new line, 8-4 with an ugly October is absolutely live, and CBS's "first team out" pick becomes the kind description. I'll take 9-3, narrowly out of the field, and a full-blown Tuscaloosa crisis of faith by Thanksgiving. **How the seat got warm:** eight losses in two years at a program that measured itself in undefeated regular seasons; a late-2025 fade; an offense that regressed; and the optics of skipping the QB portal market while rivals spent. **How he escapes:** Russell hits, Bama beats Georgia or LSU (or both), 10+ wins with a playoff appearance. Anything less and the DeBoer question stops being whispered. The nightmare scenario nobody in Alabama says out loud: 8-4 while Kiffin wins big at LSU with the roster-building model Bama declined to run.

# TIER 3 — CFP DARK HORSES

## Oklahoma Sooners

**2025:** 10-3 (6-2), made the CFP on the SEC's best defense, lost a home first-round game. **SP+:** #12, 7.3 avg wins — with the No. 3 schedule nationally. **CBS:** 8-4 (losses: at Michigan, at Georgia, Texas). **Vegas:** 7.5 (Talty: over — "too talented to win less than eight"), +1100.

**1) The roster.** QB John Mateer is back for Year 2 in the system after an injury-marred 2025 — he spent the offseason rebuilding his throwing motion after the thumb injury. The defense that ranked 4th in SP+ returns 11 of 20 rotation players: edge Taylor Wein, DT David Stone, LBs Kip Lewis and Owen Heinecke (the latter via court injunction — Oklahoma's own eligibility lawsuit win), CBs Courtland Guillory and Eli Bowen, S Peyton Bowen. Four of six D-linemen depart, but Venables has a 10-12 deep pool of blue-chip linemen to reload from. The offensive fix-it list is all portal: RB Lloyd Avant (Colorado State), five OL transfers including Arkansas starter E'Marion Harris, and a completely rebuilt WR room around returning security blanket Isaiah Sategna III — Trell Harris (Virginia), Parker Livingstone (Texas), TEs Hayden Hansen (Florida) and Rocky Beers (CSU). Watch list: Mateer's health (his style invites hits), Sategna, Stone, sophomore RB development.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals.** Offense: Ben Arbuckle's Air Raid derivative, still a work in progress — the 2025 run game ranked 124th in yards per carry, which is nearly impossible with this talent level. Defense: Venables took over play-calling in 2025 and turned it into the SEC's best unit — that's the identity, full stop. Strengths: defensive back seven, Mateer's dual-threat ceiling, Venables' scheme. Weaknesses: run game, receiver cohesion, and the honest truth about 2025 — Connelly notes OU essentially made the playoff on two defensive touchdowns and rarely looked like a top-8 team otherwise. Schedule is brutal: three projected top-15 opponents in the first five games, four straight top-20 opponents to close. Season goals: prove the CFP trip was a floor, not a fluke — and beat Texas for once (Venables is 1-3 in the Red River game).

**3) Prediction & the seat.** 8-4 feels almost predetermined by the schedule, and the regression narrative writes itself if the offense stalls again. But if Mateer stays healthy behind a functional line, this defense travels anywhere — 9-3 with wins over Ole Miss and A&M puts them right back in the bracket. **The seat:** technically cooled — a 10-win CFP season buys real credit — but the pattern is bad-good-bad-good with zero great years and zero playoff wins, and USA TODAY's hot seat piece pointedly declined to call him safe beyond "for now." Another 6-win valley and it's over; another 10-win year and he's finally built something.

## Ole Miss Rebels
