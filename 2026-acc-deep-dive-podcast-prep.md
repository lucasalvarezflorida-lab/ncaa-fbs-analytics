## Setting the Table: What Happened in 2025 (30-Second Recap)

The ACC produced the strangest championship outcome in memory and its best national result in two decades — simultaneously. Duke lost to Tulane *and* UConn out of conference, finished 6-2 in a five-way tie for second, snuck into the title game on tiebreakers, and beat Virginia in overtime to win the ACC at 9-5. The committee never even ranked the Blue Devils. Virginia — picked 14th in the preseason poll — went 7-1 to win the regular-season race outright, finished 11-3, and won the Gator Bowl after the CCG loss cost them a playoff bid.

Meanwhile, Miami took the ACC's lone CFP spot as an at-large and turned it into a run for the ages: three straight playoff wins, including Ohio State in the Cotton Bowl quarterfinal and Ole Miss in a Fiesta Bowl semifinal classic, before falling to 16-0 Indiana in the national championship game — a game the Canes led inside the final minute. Indiana's Fernando Mendoza (a Cal transfer, of all things) went No. 1 overall in the draft. Elsewhere: Clemson face-planted from preseason #4 to 7-6 (the most disappointing team in the country), FSU went 5-7 in Year 2 of its post-2023 spiral, Belichick's UNC debut was a 4-8 circus, and Virginia Tech fired Brent Pry three games in — then hired James Franklin.

**What's new for 2026:** The ACC moves to a nine-game conference schedule — except Clemson, FSU, UNC, Georgia Tech, and Boston College, who stay at eight this transition year due to existing commitments. Three new head coaches (Franklin at VT, Tosh Lupoi at Cal, Tavita Pritchard at Stanford). Week 0 gets weird: Virginia–NC State in Brazil, UNC–TCU in Ireland.

## The Playoff Picture

**The consensus:** Miami is the ACC's only true national contender, and the analysts split on whether the league gets one bid or two.

- **CBS (Brad Crawford):** Two bids — Miami runs the table (12-0), wins the ACC, and grabs the **No. 1 overall CFP seed**; SMU goes 11-1, loses the title game, and gets in at the **No. 10 seed**. Crawford has also argued Miami has a legitimate case for preseason #1 in the polls.
- **Athlon:** One bid — Miami as the **No. 7 seed**, drawn against No. 2 Georgia in a Peach Bowl quarterfinal. Their view: the ACC lacks a second team built for a deep run.
- **SP+ (Connelly):** Miami has a 28.1% shot at 11+ wins — nobody else in the league is above 3.7%. Miami also avoids two of the league's other three projected top-30 teams (no SMU, no Louisville), so it would take multiple upsets to keep the Canes out of Charlotte.
- **The betting market:** Miami -135 to win the ACC (FanDuel, July) — an enormous number for a 17-team league — with SMU +700 and Louisville +750 forming a clear second tier, then Clemson +1600.

**The kicker for the pod:** Miami has *never* won the ACC since joining in 2004 — one championship game appearance (2017, a 38-3 loss to Clemson). The heaviest conference favorite in football is chasing its first-ever league title.

# TIER 1 — THE FAVORITE

## Miami Hurricanes (2025: 13-3, 6-2 · national runner-up)

**Coach:** Mario Cristobal, Year 5 (35-19). Ice cold seat — he's coaching for a natty, not his job.

**1) Roster breakdown**

*Who left:* This is a top-heavy exodus. QB Carson Beck is out of eligibility after the title-game run. The defensive front lost its three biggest wreckers — edges Rueben Bain Jr. and Akheem Mesidor plus LB Wesley Bissainthe — and four offensive line starters are gone. WRs Keelan Marion and CJ Daniels graduated, and WR Joshisa Trader left via portal (to NC State — file that for the Wolfpack section). Overall returning production is just 49% (78th nationally), which is the one structural yellow flag on the roster.

*Who arrived (portal — No. 4 class nationally per 247Sports, only 12 commits, pure quality-over-quantity):* The headliner of the entire sport's offseason: **QB Darian Mensah from Duke** — 3,973 yards, 34 TD, 6 INT, 66.8% for the defending ACC champs, poached in a late-January portal move so telegraphed (and legally messy — the Duke NIL dispute was settled) that it instantly created the league's most bitter storyline. He brought his No. 1 target with him: **WR Cooper Barkate** (72-1,106-7 at Duke). The big-play fix was surgical: Cam Vaughn (West Virginia, 15.5 ypc) and Vandrevius Jacobs (South Carolina, 17.1 ypc) join Barkate (15.4). On defense: edge **Damon Wilson II** (Missouri — 9 sacks, elite 13.9% pressure rate) replaces Bain's juice, S Omar Thornton (BC) adds violence, and OL Jamal Meriweather (Georgia) is the lone line transfer.

*Recruits:* The crown jewel is five-star OT **Jackson Cantwell**, the nation's top tackle, who could start as a true freshman. Only ~6 redshirt freshmen on the roster — this team is built to win right now.

*Players to watch:* **Malachi Toney** is the answer to everything — 1,324 yards from scrimmage and 11 TDs as a true freshman on screens, jets, and intermediate work, plus elite punt returning, and he's already drawing Heisman dark-horse buzz. RB trio Mark Fletcher Jr. (playoff star), CharMar Brown, and Girard Pringle Jr. all return. The secondary might be the nation's best: six of eight DBs with 200+ snaps are back, all sophomores/juniors — CBs Xavier Lucas and Ethan O'Connor, safety Bryce Fitzgerald. DTs Justin Scott, Ahmad Moten Sr., and Armondo Blount anchor the interior; Marquise Lightfoot is the internal edge answer opposite Wilson.

**2) Identity, strengths, weaknesses, goals**

*Offense:* Explosive-hunting spread. Last year's attack was efficient but starved for big plays (fell from 1st to 22nd in offensive SP+); Mensah + three 15-yards-per-catch receivers is the direct fix. Connelly's read: could be *more explosive but less efficient*, because the O-line is the whole ballgame — two seasoned guards (Matthew McCoy, who may kick to tackle, and Samson Okunlola), then reserves, one transfer, and a true-freshman five-star.

*Defense:* Surged to 7th in SP+ in 2025 and carried the playoff run. The back end should be elite; the question is whether a rebuilt front six can generate anything close to last year's pass rush. If Wilson and Lightfoot approximate the Bain/Mesidor duo, this is a top-10 unit again.

*Goals:* Nothing less than the first ACC title in program history, a top-4 seed, and finishing the drive they didn't finish against Indiana. Only two real land mines: at Clemson Oct. 3 and at Notre Dame Nov. 7 (they avoid SMU and Louisville entirely).

**3) Prediction:** CBS says 12-0 and the No. 1 seed; SP+ says 9.8 wins and the league's only real playoff horse; Vegas says 10.5 and -135. Split the difference: 11-1 or better, ACC championship game, playoff lock barring a Mensah injury. **Coach status:** untouchable.

# TIER 2 — THE CHASE PACK

## SMU Mustangs (2025: 9-4, 6-2)

**Coach:** Rhett Lashlee, Year 5 (38-17). Secure — 14-2 in ACC regular-season play since joining, 31 wins over three years.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The great news: **Kevin Jennings returns as a third-year starting QB**, a genuine rarity. The sobering news: only eight returning starters, the top four yards-from-scrimmage producers are gone, and seven defensive starters left. Portal class was lean by design (15 in, ~two dozen out — a sign of roster health): RB Kendrick Raphael (Cal), WRs Yannick Smith (ECU) and Jalen Hale (Alabama), TEs Randy Pittman Jr. (FSU) and Theo Melin Öhrström (Texas A&M), edges Marques White (UMass, 10.5 TFL) and Ira Singleton (USF), nickel Jarvis Lee (USF, 9.5 TFL), plus 305-pound-plus DTs Christian Davis (La. Tech) and Malcolm Alcorn-Crowder (K-State). Returners to watch: All-ACC OT **PJ Williams** fronts a line that needed zero portal help (a luxury almost nobody else in this league has), slot Yamir Knight, sophomores Derrick McFall (RB) and Jalen Cooper (WR), three returning LBs (Booker, Miyazono, Kilgore) and three returning corners.

**2) Identity:** Lashlee's tempo spread, now run by a *committee* — he lost both coordinators (OC Casey Woods to the Missouri State HC job, DC Scott Symons to the Cowboys) and responded by naming co-coordinator troikas: Rob Likens/Garin Justice/D'Eriq King on offense, Maurice Crum Jr./Rickey Hunley Jr. on defense. Either supreme cultural confidence or overthinking — that's a live pod debate. Jennings is the whole personality: in 26 starts, SMU is 12-1 when his QBR clears 76 and 3-4 when it dips under 50. Strengths: QB continuity, the O-line, linebacker depth. Weaknesses: brand-new skill corps chemistry, a defense in transition post-Symons, and special teams (102nd last year). Goal: get back to Charlotte after blowing it with kick-yourself losses at Wake (13-12) and Cal (38-35) — beat either and they'd have played for the title.

**3) Prediction:** CBS's boldest call in the league — 11-1, ACC runner-up, No. 10 CFP seed, aided by a schedule with **no Miami** and early road tests at FSU and Louisville. SP+ is cooler (8.0 wins). The Sept. 19 trip to Louisville is the biggest non-Miami game on the ACC calendar. **Coach status:** completely safe.

## Louisville Cardinals (2025: 9-4, 4-4)

**Coach:** Jeff Brohm, Year 4 (28-12). Secure — three straight 9+ win seasons, but the "what-if" file is thick.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The Brohm formula continues: 30+ transfers, new QB, stable 9-win product. QB Miller Moss is gone; former Ohio State backup **Lincoln Kienholz** takes over (Davin Wydner of West Georgia behind him). The one elite returning unit: RBs **Isaac Brown and Keyjuan Brown**, arguably the country's best duo — 1,588 combined yards at 8.1 per carry with zero QB run threat helping them. The receiver room is fully rebuilt: Tre Richardson (Vanderbilt — big-play threat and dangerous kick returner), Lawayne McCoy (FSU), Jackson Voth (Drake), TEs Brody Foley (Tulsa) and Justyn Reid (Tulane). O-line: only guard Lance Robinson returns; five transfers arrive, led by All-CUSA T Anwar O'Neal (Delaware) and All-Sun Belt T Johnnie Brown III (Georgia Southern). Defense returns real stars — edge Clev Lubin (8.5 sacks), LB Stanquan Clark, nickel Antonio Watts (8.5 TFL), CB Tayon Holloway — but only 5 of 16 players with 200+ snaps, so 14 defensive transfers (Iowa S Koen Entringer, Tennessee S Kaleb Beasley, Kentucky CB DJ Waller Jr., Purdue DT Demeco Kennedy, UNC edge Tyler Thompson among them) fill the gaps under new co-DCs Steve Ellis and Mark Ivey. Portal class ranked ~18th nationally.

**2) Identity:** Brohm's aggressive, QB-friendly pass game — though with this RB room and an unproven QB, expect a more ground-forward tilt early. Strengths: backfield, edge rush, Brohm's track record of making portal rosters click. Weaknesses: Kienholz is the least-proven QB Brohm has started here, the OL is transfer-dependent, and the margins are razor thin — they've lost by combined single digits in the games that kept them out of Charlotte two straight years (three 2025 ACC losses by a combined 7 points). Goal: stop losing coin-flips and finally reach the title game.

**3) Prediction:** CBS has 9-3 with a monster 8-1 ACC mark; SP+ notes they're projected favorites in every game after the Ole Miss opener (a Sunday game in Nashville) but play *eight* projected one-score games. They don't face Miami, Clemson, or Virginia, and get SMU and Pitt at home. If Kienholz is good, they're a title-game team. **Coach status:** safe, and per CBS one of the sport's most underrated coaches.

## Clemson Tigers (2025: 7-6, 4-4)

**Coach:** Dabo Swinney, Year 18 (186-53). Not on the hot seat — but on a hot-seat *list* ("others receiving votes" per Athlon) for the first time in his career. That's the story.

**1) Roster breakdown:** A genuine reset. Gone: QB Cade Klubnik, three top-50 NFL draft picks off the defense, star OT Blake Miller, five of the top seven defensive linemen, and seven offensive starters overall. The offense belongs to junior **Christopher Vizzina** — a two-year backup who looked solid in his one career start — with true freshman Tait Reynolds lurking. Skill talent is real if healthy: WRs Bryant Wesco Jr. (injured most of '25) and T.J. Moore, RB Gideon Davidson plus SMU transfer Chris Johnson Jr. Five of the nine linemen who started last year (injuries everywhere) are back. Dabo finally leaned into the portal — 10 transfers, a career high though still below the national average — headlined by edge London Merritt (Colorado), DT Markus Strong (Oklahoma), and safeties Corey Myrick (Southern Miss) and Jerome Carter (Old Dominion). Players to watch: DE **Will Heldt** (17.5 TFLs — a legit All-America candidate) and the LB duo of **Sammy Brown** and Kobe McCloud, maybe the ACC's best.

**2) Identity:** Here's the spicy part — the offense is going *backward to go forward*: **Chad Morris returns as OC**, the man who modernized Clemson from 2011-14 but hasn't coordinated a good offense since 2017. Skeptics see a regressive hire; believers see a culture fit who can build a clean, QB-friendly system around Vizzina. Defensively they've been less than the sum of their parts for three straight years (outside the SP+ top 20 each season despite blue-chip talent). Strengths: front-seven star power, home-heavy early schedule (five of the first seven at home). Weaknesses: unproven QB, rebuilt secondary, big-play allowance, and the psychological residue of 2025. Goal: a redemption arc — get back to Charlotte and quiet the "is the dynasty over" chorus.

**3) Prediction:** Fascinating split — SP+ ranks Clemson 23rd, *second-best in the ACC*, while Vegas lumps them at 7.5 wins with the middle class and CBS says 8-4 (6-2) with losses to LSU, Miami, Virginia Tech, and South Carolina. They're the league's hinge team: the Oct. 3 Miami visit and Oct. 31 trip to FSU will decide whether this is a bounce-back or a slow burn.

**4) The seat, such as it is:** How he got here: three straight years of defensive underachievement, a 2025 collapse from #4 to 7-6 (worst since 2010), and a stubborn late arrival to portal-era roster building. How he gets out: it doesn't take a title — a 9-or-10-win season with a competent Vizzina and a top-15 defense resets the entire narrative, and the schedule gives him a 5-2-or-better runway. What keeps him safe regardless: the résumé, the buyout, and an administration with no appetite for a divorce.

# TIER 3 — THE FASCINATING MIDDLE

## Virginia Tech Hokies (2025: 3-9, 2-6)

**Coach:** James Franklin, Year 1. Obviously safe — this is the league's most anticipated debut.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The weirdest stat in the conference: a program that just fired its coach ranks **4th nationally in returning production (69%)**. Most of a very young O-line returns, plus DT Kemari Copeland, LB Kaleb Spencer, and scrappy corners Isaiah Brown-Murray and Thomas Williams. Franklin then imported State College wholesale: QB **Ethan Grunkemeyer** (85.2 QBR and 76% completions over his last three PSU starts), TE Luke Reynolds, and OC Ty Howle all followed him, and the recruiting class is loaded with flipped Penn State commits. The portal haul (ESPN's #14 class) is deep: CBs Jaquez White (Troy — 3 INTs, 12 PBUs, Connelly's pick as the class's biggest immediate-impact guy) and Cam Chadwick (UConn), young ex-blue-chip edges Javion Hilson (Missouri) and Mylachi Williams (PSU), RB Bill Davis (Louisiana), WRs Que'Sean Brown (Duke) and Marlion Jackson (La. Tech). Watch: RB Marcellous Hawkins and Davis are both elite yards-after-contact runners.

**2) Identity:** Penn State South — efficient, physical, TE-friendly, run-anchored, and yes, occasionally conservative. The twist of the year: **Brent Pry, the fired head coach, stayed on as Franklin's defensive coordinator.** Freed from CEO duties, Pry gets to just coach a unit that underachieved last year. Strengths: continuity nobody expected, a semi-proven QB, a soft on-ramp (no projected top-30 opponent until Oct. 24 at Clemson). Weaknesses: receiver proof, and the usual Year 1 alignment wobbles. Goal: 7-8 wins, restore the identity, and let the blue-chip recruiting wave land for 2027.

**3) Prediction:** CBS says 8-4 (5-4); SP+ says 7.3 wins against a 6.5 Vegas total. The Oct. 24 Clemson trip and the season-ending Commonwealth Clash vs. Virginia are the measuring sticks. An 8-win debut makes Franklin the story of the ACC's middle class.

## Virginia Cavaliers (2025: 11-3, 7-2 — regular-season champs, CCG runner-up)

**Coach:** Tony Elliott, Year 5 (22-26). Fully off the hot seat — last year flipped his tenure.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The 2025 miracle was a portal Hail Mary that landed — and now they have to do it again. Only 16 of the 40 players who logged 200+ snaps return, so Elliott signed **30 more transfers** (Connelly ranked it among his 20 favorite classes). QB Chandler Morris (3,000 yards) is gone; **Beau Pribula** (Missouri, ex-Penn State) takes over. The additions skew explosive-on-bad-teams: RBs Jekail Middlebrook (MTSU) and Solomon Beebe (UAB), WRs Rico Flores Jr. (UCLA) and Da'Shawn Martin (Kent State), plus Connelly's favorite big man in the class, DT **Zion Wilson** (ECU — 9.5 TFLs, 7 sacks), and ten defenders with FBS starts. The returning core is legit: third-team All-ACC linemen Noah Josey and McKale Boley front what's billed as the most experienced O-line in the country, plus DE Fisher Camac, CBs Jam Jackson and Donavon Platt, and LB Kam Robinson. Sustainability note: only 14 of the 30 transfers are seniors — a first attempt at stability.

**2) Identity:** Ball control + a defense that jumped from 86th to 19th in SP+. Strengths: O-line, secondary experience, and motivation — the OT title-game loss to Duke cost them a playoff berth and they have not forgotten. Weaknesses: total dependence on portal chemistry for the second straight year, and a QB downgrade until proven otherwise. Goal: prove 2025 was a program change, not a lightning strike, and get back to Charlotte.

**3) Prediction:** The nerds and the humans disagree: SP+ has them 40th with regression baked in; CBS projects 7-5 (4-5). But there's not a single projected top-30 opponent on the schedule, so if this transfer class hits at even 80% of last year's rate, 9+ wins is live. Week 0 vs. NC State *in Brazil* is a wild opener. **Coach status:** ice cold.

## Florida State Seminoles (2025: 5-7, 2-6) — THE hot seat team

**Coach:** Mike Norvell, Year 7 (38-34). **CBS hot-seat rating: 5.0 — unanimous, the hottest seat in college football** (shared only with Wisconsin's Fickell and Baylor's Aranda).

**1) Roster breakdown:** Tommy Castellanos is gone after the classic Castellanos arc (torched projections by 17 points a game through September, faded hard after). New QB: **Ashton Daniels** (Auburn, ex-Stanford) — steadier passer, less explosive, physical runner. The skill core is genuinely good: RB **Ousmane Kromah** (breakout candidate) plus Texas transfer Quintrevion Wisner, and WRs **Duce Robinson and Micahi Danzy** both rank among the ACC's top six returning receivers in yards per route. The terrifying part: the **top seven offensive linemen are all gone** — five transfers arrived (All-MAC T Nate Pabst, Auburn's Xavier Chaplin) and up to four could start. Defense: LB duo Blake Nichelson + Southern Miss tackle-machine Chris Jones should make the run D nasty; DTs Daniel Lyons and Jordan Sanders push the pocket; but sophomore Mandrell Desir is the only proven pass rusher and the secondary lost four of its top six (including All-ACC S Earl Little Jr.). Portal class ranked 26th nationally / 5th in the ACC — and notably, the biggest "wins" were retentions of young talent.

**2) Identity:** New wrinkle — Gus Malzahn retired in February, receivers coach Tim Harris Jr. was promoted to OC, and Norvell is taking a heavier hand in his own offense. Expect his up-tempo, RPO-heavy system with more QB run. Tony White's 3-3-5 improved in bend-don't-break fashion (54th in success rate allowed, but 6th in yards allowed per successful play). Strengths: skill talent, linebackers, run defense. Weaknesses: a fully transfer-built O-line (the single most common failure mode in college football), pass rush, secondary depth, and a psyche that's absorbed two years of losing. Goals: honestly? Survive. No road win since 2023 — get one. Get bowl-eligible early, then steal one of the big ones.

**3) Prediction:** SP+ actually respects the roster (35th, 6.6 wins) but the schedule is a meat grinder — SMU, Alabama, Louisville, and Miami all by mid-October, Clemson on Halloween, Florida to close. CBS projects 6-6 (4-4) with losses to every good team on the slate.

**4) The seat — how he got here and how he escapes:** The arc is brutal: 13-0 in 2023 → the CFP snub → 2-10 in 2024 → a 5-7 "rebound" in 2025 that still meant 7-17 across two years with zero ACC relevance. FSU openly circled the Lane Kiffin drama in November and reportedly would have moved if it materialized; the $50M+ buyout is widely described as the only reason he's employed. Multiple outlets call 2026 make-or-break, some flatly a foregone conclusion that it's his last year. His path off: (1) the transfer O-line must gel immediately, because everything else follows from it; (2) Daniels has to be the stabilizer Castellanos wasn't in November; (3) beat *one* of Alabama/Miami/Clemson/Florida to change the emotional math; (4) win road games, plural. Realistically, 8-4 with a signature scalp saves him; 6-6 with losses to every ranked team means Tallahassee is hiring in December.

## Pittsburgh Panthers (2025: 8-5, 6-2)

**Coach:** Pat Narduzzi, Year 12 (80-61). Lukewarm — no formal danger, but patience isn't infinite.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The crown jewel returns: **Mason Heintschel**, who took over as a true freshman and threw for 2,354 yards and 16 TDs in 10 games (9 starts). Both lines are loaded — five of the top seven OL are back plus All-MAC G Keylen Davis (Akron), and the DL returns ends Isaiah Neal and Jimmy Scott with DT Nick James. The exodus is out wide and in the back: six of the top seven receivers gone, five of the top eight DBs gone, and star LB Rasheem Biles left for Texas. Adds: RB La'Vell Wright (WKU) to pair with sophomore Ja'Kyrian Turner, FCS star WR Malik Knight (Western Carolina), TEs Elijah Lagg (UAB) and Carson Kent (Oklahoma), nickel DeMarco Ward (Memphis), CB Raion Strader. Watch: WR Cataurus Hicks is the lone proven pass catcher; young DBs Shadarian Harrison, Shawn Lee Jr., Cruce Brookins, and Josh Guerrier all got baptism-by-injury reps last year.

**2) Identity:** Vintage Narduzzi — a top-5-nationally run defense (25.8% stuff rate, 3.6 yards per carry allowed) attached to a leaky pass defense (82nd in completion rate allowed), now paired with a legitimately modern QB. 2025 Pitt was the platonic borderline top-40 team: 7-1 against everyone outside the SP+ top 40, 1-4 against it. Strengths: Heintschel, both lines, run D. Weaknesses: receiver proof, secondary rebuild, and a schedule with road trips to Virginia Tech, Miami, Louisville, and Cal. Goal: turn Heintschel Year 2 into the top-20 breakthrough this program keeps flirting with.

**3) Prediction:** The widest spread in the league: Vegas 7.5, SP+ 6.9 — and CBS at a pessimistic 6-6 (3-6) with a possible four-game skid to close and zero signature wins. If the young DBs grow up fast, the over hits easily; if not, it's a grumpy November on Cardiac Hill.

## NC State Wolfpack (2025: 8-5, 4-4)

**Coach:** Dave Doeren, Year 14 (95-70). Warm — not listed, but the Year 14 ceiling conversation is live.

**1) Roster breakdown:** Start here: **CJ Bailey is the best returning QB in the ACC by Total QBR** and is already climbing 2027 draft boards. Around him, though, it's a gut renovation — top rusher, top four pass catchers (the leading WR transferred to USC), and three OL starters all gone. Doeren signed eight skill transfers, headlined by WRs Victor Snow (Buffalo) and **Joshisa Trader (Miami)** — yes, a Canes-to-Pack storyline. The line adds an All-AAC gem in T Jimarion McCrimon (ECU — one sack allowed all year; Connelly called proven OL transfers "gold" and this one of the class's best) plus 6-8, 345-pound FCS mauler Jai'Lun Hampton. Defense: the pass rush was nonexistent, so edge Harvey Dyson (Tulane — 8 sacks for the American champs) and 336-pound DT Katron Evans (Marshall) arrive, with PSU S King Mack, UNC DB Ty White, and Georgia DB Ondre Evans joining a secondary that returns six 200-snap players, mostly sophomores/juniors.

**2) Identity:** Bailey's improvisation — a scrambler who rarely takes sacks — behind a huge line, attached to a defense that's ranked outside the SP+ top 60 two straight years (the program's historical identity, inverted). Strengths: QB, O-line size, young DB continuity. Weaknesses: pass rush until proven, brand-new receiver room, and a front-loaded schedule: Virginia in Brazil (Week 0), Vanderbilt, and Louisville all by Oct. 3 — though all three break in new QBs. Goal: turn the late-2025 surge (4-4 → 8-5) into a 9-win year and shut down the ceiling talk.

**3) Prediction:** SP+ 6.9 wins, Vegas 7.5, CBS a deflating 6-6 (4-5). The range of outcomes is enormous because Bailey alone is worth two wins. **Coach status:** another .500 season "would inevitably spark tougher questions" — CBS's words, and Raleigh's mood.

## Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets (2025: 9-4, 6-2)

**Coach:** Brent Key, Year 5 (27-20). Secure after three straight winning seasons.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The Haynes King era is over (7,907 passing / 2,277 rushing yards as a Jacket — a human third-down conversion), and the turnover is extreme: only **3 of 16 offensive players with 200+ snaps return** (RB Malachi Hosley, OTs Ethan Mackenny and Malachi Carney), and the defense lost DT Jordan van den Berg plus five of its top six DBs. Both coordinators left too (OC Buster Faulkner to Florida, DC Blake Gideon to Texas). The reload is fun, though: QB **Alberto Mendoza** (Indiana — Fernando's little brother, who posted a 99.4 QBR in 102 garbage-time snaps: 18-of-24, 286 yards, 5 TDs, plus 190 rushing yards on 13 carries) and RB **Justice Haynes** (Michigan — 857 yards at 7.1 a pop and 10 TDs in just seven games). Key's portal tells you his priorities: six defensive linemen and two corners, including UConn's Vincent Carroll-Jackson, Colorado's Tawfiq Thomas, and ex-Bama blue-chips Noah Carter (edge) and Jaylen Mbakwe (CB). Watch: LB E.J. Lightsey is the one proven defensive anchor; 6-4 Elon WR Isaiah Fuhrmann needs to get healthy because the pass-catching cupboard is nearly bare.

**2) Identity:** Line-of-scrimmage football — Key is an O-lineman to his core — now with an NFL flavor: new OC George Godsey (a GT alum back after 15 NFL seasons) and new DC Jason Semore (Marshall/Southern Miss). Expect a Haynes/Hosley-powered run game with play-action shots and a defense betting on unlocked athleticism. Strengths: backfield, tackles, elite special teams (11th in SP+). Weaknesses: receiver room, secondary rebuild, proven defensive bodies generally. Goal: keep the winning-season streak alive in an obvious reset year and set up a legitimate 2027 run.

**3) Prediction:** CBS 7-5 (5-3) feels right; SP+ is harsher (43rd, 6.1) mostly on schedule (Tennessee, at Georgia, Louisville, at Clemson, at Virginia Tech). A fourth straight winning season here would be quietly excellent coaching. **Coach status:** cold seat, warm buzz.

## Duke Blue Devils (2025: 9-5, 7-2 — ACC CHAMPIONS)

Goal: defend the title with a straight face — and win the revenge game when **Miami visits Durham**, the pettiest date on the ACC calendar.

**3) Prediction:** CBS says 6-6 (4-5); SP+ says 6.5 wins but notes six projected one-score ACC games — Duke overachieved projections by 12+ points five times last year, so writing them off is how you get burned twice. **Coach status:** cold.

## California Golden Bears (2025: 7-6, 4-4)

## Wake Forest Demon Deacons (2025: 9-4, 4-4)

## North Carolina Tar Heels (2025: 4-8, 2-6)

**Coach:** Bill Belichick, Year 2 (4-8). **Hot seat: 4.1 CBS rating, Athlon top-10.**

**1) Roster breakdown:** Year 1's fatal flaw was a transfer class that Belichick and GM Michael Lombardi badly overrated (the "33rd NFL team" quote became a national punchline), leaving UNC 124th in offensive SP+. The Year 2 rebuild looks more coherent — though returning production is dead last in the ACC at 44%. QB: **Billy Edwards Jr.** (productive at Maryland, lost most of 2025 to injury at Wisconsin) — debatable ceiling, much higher floor. RB Demon June and slot Jordan Shipp are real returning pieces; the line adds size in JacQawn McRoy (Arkansas) and Andrew Threatt (Charleston Southern); and in peak Belichick fashion, he signed three transfer tight ends and a Patriot League star WR (Lehigh's Mason Humphrey). Defense was the 2025 bright spot under Steve Belichick (59th SP+, disciplined bend-don't-break): edge **Melkart Abou Jaoude** (10.5 sacks) headlines, with Leroy Jackson, Isaiah Johnson, and nickel Kaleb Cost back, plus a Richmond pipeline (edge Donovan Hoilette Jr., LB Peyton Seelmann).

**2) Identity:** The real hire is **OC Bobby Petrino** — whatever else you think of him, he has demonstrably improved recent offenses at Texas A&M and Arkansas, and pairing his scheme with Belichick's situational defense is at minimum fascinating television. Strengths: defensive discipline, coaching IQ in one-score games, a competent-or-better QB for the first time in the era. Weaknesses: zero proven OL, thin receiver talent, roster churn, and the noise machine. Goal per CBS: bowl eligibility = tangible, honest progress.

**3) Prediction:** SP+ 58th, 5.3 wins; CBS 6-6 (4-4). The schedule guarantees main-character status immediately: TCU in Ireland (Week 0), at Clemson (Week 3), Notre Dame (Week 5). Win one of those and it's "Belichick redemption" headlines; start 1-3 and it's retirement-watch content by October.

**4) The seat:** How he got here: the most hyped hire in college football history producing a team that didn't beat a single FBS opponent with a winning record, wrapped in a year of off-field soap opera (the exit rumors he had to call "categorically false," the Lombardi discourse, the general circus). How he escapes: let Petrino run the offense without interference, ride the defense, win the winnable middle of the schedule, and — bluntly — keep the program out of the tabloids. Six or seven wins with visible discipline probably buys Year 3. Another 4-8 and both sides likely negotiate the exit everyone half-expects.

## Syracuse Orange (2025: 3-9, 1-7)

**Coach:** Fran Brown, Year 3 (13-12). Warm — the 10-2 debut equity is real but depleted.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The season died in September: Steve Angeli (4 solid starts) went down against Clemson, and Syracuse averaged 11.1 points the rest of the way while losing eight straight by an average of 27.5. The fix is a genuine three-man QB derby — **Angeli is back but Brown has pointedly not named him the starter**, with dual-threat Amari Odom (Kennesaw State) and former five-star Malachi Nelson (UTEP) brought in to push him. Around them: RB Ahmad Miller (Jackson State), sized-up WRs Cole Weaver (Miami-OH), Elijah Moore (FSU), and Matt Outten (PSU), TE Noah Meyers (WKU), and an O-line returning five players with starting experience averaging 6-6, 331 — mostly underclassmen. He inherits aggressive corners Demetres Samuel Jr. and Chris Peal and imports pass rush: FCS All-American Keyshawn Johnson (UT Martin, 13.5 sacks), Tunmise Adeleye (UNLV), and 300-pound Jartavius Flounoy (Georgia State).

**2) Identity:** Brown's brand is swagger, recruiting, and toughness; Kehres brings structure to a defense that has regressed 20+ SP+ spots three straight years — Connelly is confident that streak ends even if a leap doesn't come. Strengths: secondary, young OL beef, chip-on-shoulder energy. Weaknesses: QB uncertainty, skill-position proof, and the ACC's new nine-game gauntlet. ## Boston College Eagles (2025: 2-10, 1-7)

**Coach:** Bill O'Brien, Year 3 (9-16). **Hot seat: top-10 nationally (Athlon #7-range).**

**1) Roster breakdown:** The response to a lost season was drastic: O'Brien fired the structure — he's now **his own offensive coordinator**, with 62-year-old Ted Roof running the defense. The transfer class is genuinely intriguing for this level: RB **Evan Dickens** (Liberty) rushed for 1,339 yards — ninth nationally despite 11 games — with 3.8 yards after contact per carry; Connelly called him an outright steal. QB is the wild card: **Mason McKenzie**, a dual-threat jumping all the way from Division II Saginaw Valley State, had an up-and-down spring. The line gets G6 starters Reggie Jackson (Jacksonville State) and Owen Snively (Eastern Michigan); Colgate WR Reed Swanson and TE Kaelan Chudzinski round out the weapons. Defense adds LB Anthony Palano (Washington State), Harvard edge Alex DeGrieck, and ex-blue-chips Kris Jones (Georgia) and KJ Sampson (FSU) up front — but the returning secondary helped BC rank 126th in yards per dropback, and Roof hasn't run a strong defense since the 2010s.

**2) Identity:** The plan is transparent — physical, Dickens-powered, clock-controlling offense to protect a D2 quarterback and a bad defense. Strengths: Dickens, tight end, special teams (28th). Weaknesses: nearly everything else — pass protection, pass defense, and depth. Goal: competence. Non-conference trips to Cincinnati and Rutgers will tell you everything by mid-September.

**3) Prediction:** CBS's most brutal projection in the league: **1-11, 0-8** — a winless ACC slate. SP+ is gentler (4.0 wins) but 74th overall.

**4) The seat:** How he got here: the 4-1 start of 2024 created belief; BC is 5-15 since, with the offense imploding via sacks/turnovers and a defense that stopped doing anything well. Two years in with regression, at a program that can't out-spend its mistakes. How he escapes: the identity has to work immediately — feed Dickens 25 times a game, shorten games, steal the Rutgers/Cincinnati tossups, and scrap to 4-5 wins with visible fight. Anything resembling that CBS projection ends the era; even Saturday Down South framed it plainly — below .500 brings the real-estate agents.

## Stanford Cardinal (2025: 4-8, 3-5)

**Coach:** Tavita Pritchard, Year 1. New hire — the seat question is years away.

**1) Roster breakdown:** The full context: Stanford has been the worst power-conference program of the 2020s — 7-39 against teams ranked better than 80th over five years, average SP+ ranking of 102.8, no winning full season since 2018. GM Andrew Luck doubled down on the family tree: Pritchard (former Stanford QB and longtime assistant, most recently Commanders QB coach) as HC, former Stanford OL coach Terry Heffernan as OC, with ex-Seahawks DC Kris Richard the one outside hire. The roster has individual bright spots — RB Micah Ford (strong after contact), G Simione Pale (back from injury; excellent in 2024), tackling machine LB Matt Rose (a stop every 6.1 snaps), and CB **Brandon Nicholson**, one of the ACC's best. But the most proven receiver is a Yale transfer (Nico Brown), the DL replaces four of six, and QB is 24-year-old Michigan transfer **Davis Warren** — a respected leader with a career line of 5.4 yards per dropback and 7 TDs to 10 INTs.

**2) Identity:** The stated dream is restoring the Harbaugh/Shaw power-run DNA — the problem being Stanford ranked 132nd at 3.9 yards per non-sack carry last year, so the identity currently exists only in the brochure. Strengths: linebacker/secondary experience, culture continuity, house money. Weaknesses: line play on both sides, QB ceiling, skill talent, kicking game (128th ST). Goal: be competitive, win the games you should (Hawaii and Elon are must-haves), and start recruiting like the plan is real.

**3) Prediction:** CBS 2-10 (0-9); SP+ 3.9 wins vs. a 3.5 total. This is a foundation-pouring year, full stop. **Coach status:** safe by definition.
